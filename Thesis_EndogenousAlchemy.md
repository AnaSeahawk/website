To mathematically map the biophysical geometry of internal alchemy, the following protocol establishes strict volumetric, thermal, and electrochemical controls for the distillation process.

* **Volumetric Baseline:** Before distillation, the exact starting volume of the aged plasma must be measured and recorded using a graduated glass vessel. 

### 2. The Apparatus and Thermal Constraints
* **The Chamber:** A standard 3000ml stainless steel distillation unit is utilized. 
* **The 50% Rule:** The chamber must never be filled past 50% capacity (1500ml maximum) to prevent the dense, unrefined matrix from boiling over into the cooling coils and contaminating the volatile fractions.
* **Thermal Application:** Standard low-heat distillation is applied. 

### 3. The Fractional Collection Protocol
The distillation yields three distinct biophysical phases, which must be collected in separate, clearly marked vessels. Precise timestamps must be recorded at the shift between each phase.

* **Fraction 1: The Plasma Spirit (The Volatile Purifier)**
  * **Observation:** The first fluid to breach the cooling coil, presenting as a heavy, foaming overflow driven by the vaporization of electron-dense gases. 
  * **Collection:** Capture this volatile fraction until the heavy foaming ceases and the output transitions to a steady, clear drip. Record the yield and timestamp.
* **Fraction 2: The Clear Water (The Structural Medium)**
  * **Observation:** The refined, structurally stable, clear aqueous output.
  * **Collection:** Capture this fraction until the primary chamber has yielded the majority of its fluid. Record the yield.
* **Fraction 3: The Earth Matrix (The Lunar Retentate)**
  * **Observation:** The dense, dark, non-volatile concentrate remaining in the primary chamber. 
  * **Collection:** Allow the chamber to cool slightly, then collect this thick matrix. Record the final volume.

### 4. Electrochemical Analysis

* **pH:** To measure the structural alkaline voltage.

* **H2 (Hydrogen) & TDS:** To measure gas concentration and dense mineral capacity.
