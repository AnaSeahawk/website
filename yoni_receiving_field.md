## Research orientation

This book is a field-based inquiry into sexuality as a somatic, imaginal, and civilizational intelligence.

It is not ideological.  
It is not therapeutic.  
It is not instructional.  
It is not corrective.

It assumes the reader is already perceptive, already questioning, already aware that something essential has been lost.

Primary premise:  
Sexuality itself is not broken.  
The conditions that once oriented it have collapsed.

This book does not seek to repair sexuality, but to restore the field through which eros can once again settle, complete, and belong to life.

---

## Core thesis (held quietly throughout)

Modern sexuality is saturated with stimulation and starved of containment.  
What has been lost is not freedom, but completion.

Women are not called here to reclaim sexuality, but to reconstitute the receptive field through which eros regains coherence.

This is leadership through reception, not assertion.
