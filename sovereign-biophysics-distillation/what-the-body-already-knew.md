# What the Body Already Knew

## A practitioner's account of three years with the distilled fractions

*Ana Seahawk — Sarande, Albania — May 2026*

---

I am writing this from Sarande, Albania, on the sixth day before we move again — this time uphill, toward the sea. My period is coming. I can feel it the way I have learned to feel things again: from the inside, with some accuracy, with a kind of welcome. That relationship with my own body is itself the subject of this entry.

---

## The Beginning: What I Was Doing to Myself

I took the birth control pill for most of my adult reproductive life. Not continuously without interruption, but substantially. From after the birth of my son in 1998, through years of travel, through my nomadic life with Olivier, I was on the pill more often than I was off it. By 2011, during an extended period of travel, I was taking it almost continuously — specifically so that I would not get my period. I did not want the inconvenience. I did not understand what I was suppressing.

The pill works by flattening the hypothalamic-pituitary-gonadal axis into silence. It delivers a synthetic hormonal signal that tells the body: no need to cycle. No need to produce your own estrogen and progesterone in the intricate, phase-specific rhythms that define female biology. What I experienced as convenience was the suppression of one of the most sophisticated self-regulatory systems in biology. I was quiet about it. I barely noticed. That is how effective the suppression was.

I stopped the pill in 2022, just before Olivier and I went vegetarian and just before I began shivambu. It was not a coordinated plan. It converged.

## Shivambu and the Concentrate Practice

We began the amaroli practice on April 3, 2022, in Laos. Drinking first. Then, after about a year, we read the Damar Tantra's instructions for the matured and reduced preparation — and began open evaporation: reducing the collected urine on the stovetop in an open pot until the volume was significantly reduced. No still. No fractions. What remained was a concentrated essence — thick, dark, mineral-dense — and this is what we have been applying topically for approximately three years.

Olivier and I came to this together. We discovered shivambu at the same time, read the same texts — the Damar Tantra, the traditions of amaroli, the Taoist and tantric streams around semen retention — and began the practice simultaneously. Four years of reading and learning, side by side. We started with separate waters, each working with our own material. That continued for the first few years. We began combining for the serum in 2025. With the distiller came the next step: since February 2026, we combine everything — the drinking, the Spirit, the Essence, the serum, all of it. The first two distillation runs documented in this archive were run separately as isolated records; since then it has been combined. In the traditions we were reading, the exchange of biological essences between partners has its own logic: Shiva and Parvati, the masculine and feminine principles in dynamic interplay, each carrying something the other metabolises differently. When the combining came, it came naturally.

It was in Torremolinos, Spain — early 2026 — that it became obvious to me that the countertop water distiller we had was already doing what I wanted, just for water. In February 2026, I ran the first proper distillation. What came back was not one concentrated fraction but three — and what I had been doing with the open evaporation for three years was a rougher version of the same thing: the volatile compounds had been escaping into the kitchen air, the Spirit uncaptured, while the concentrated Essence and mineral retentate remained in the pot. The distiller separated what the open pot had collapsed together.

The three-fraction vocabulary — Spirit, Essence, and Serum — belongs to the distillation practice that began in February 2026. Spirit names the solar distillate: the risen, light, volatile fraction that comes over with the water. Essence names the lunar middle fraction: transitional, relational. Serum names the Saturn retentate: concentrated, fixed, dense with biological information. The three-year topical practice preceded this framework; the framework now names what the body was already receiving.

I want to be precise about what those three years meant. We were not performing a controlled experiment. We were living a practice. The concentrate was part of how we began and ended each day. It was woven into our physical care of each other and of ourselves. The volumes were significant. We have not been conservative about any of this.

## What We Noticed

The skin changed. Sun tolerance improved. The cycle continues at 52 — arriving on time, light, with a quality of interoception that the years on the pill had gradually taken away. The sexual life deepened. The relationship deepened with it. There is a particular quality of being known by someone whose biological information you have been receiving and returning — whose hormonal language you have been speaking on the skin — that is not easily described in the language available to us. The love has a different texture than it had before the practice.

The practice of karezza — conscious non-goal-oriented union, drawn from the same confluence of tantric and Taoist streams that inform amaroli — names something of what became available. What Stockham described in 1896 as the overflow quality of union: satiation without depletion. The body signals completion without requiring ejaculation. That state became increasingly available with practice. It is not metaphor.

## The Nomadic Practice: Starting with What You Have

We have built and maintained this practice across multiple countries, in rented apartments, on a budget, without permanence. In Torremolinos we have three 20-litre containers of raw plasma in storage — one from each of us separately, one combined — approximately 60 litres of aged material waiting for the next distillation runs when we return. We also have several litres in storage in Beloeil, Quebec, from the earlier phase of the practice, collected under a different dietary signature: vegan, fruit and vegetable rich, no sodium chloride. Not ideal containers. Not glass, not the amber vessels the tradition recommends.

I want to be direct about this because I hear the same barrier from people who are drawn to the practice: I do not have the right equipment. I am waiting until I have glass. I am waiting until I have a proper still. I am waiting for the permanent home.

The body does not wait for perfect conditions. The practice does not require them. We have been using yoghurt jars for the distilled Spirit waters. We have been distilling in whatever vessel the current city provides. The biological intelligence of the material is not contingent on the perfection of the container.

Mary the Jewess did not wait for borosilicate glass. The tradition encoded in the Damar Tantra does not specify pharmaceutical-grade storage. In Shivambu Kalpa Vidhi, the instruction to reduce urine to one quarter of its volume in an earthen pot and use the matured preparation for massage is not a preamble to a specification sheet. It is a practical instruction for a practice that belongs to the body, not to the laboratory.

Start. Observe. Adjust. The body is the instrument. Everything else is secondary.

---

At 52, cycling regularly, with strong skin and a sexual life of depth and sustained vitality, after decades of pill use and a decade of intensive hormonal suppression, after four years of this practice in rented rooms across multiple countries, with yoghurt jars and practical containers — I am the data.

The female body is the original laboratory. It does not require permission to run its experiments. It requires only that we stop interrupting it.
