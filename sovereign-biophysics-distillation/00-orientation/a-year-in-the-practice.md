# A Year in the Practice

*One cycle is enough to begin. One full year is enough to understand what you are working with.*

---

## Why a Year

The body does not repeat itself exactly. It runs cycles — the lunar month, the seasonal quarter, the solar year — and each cycle has its own character. What you distill in winter is different from what you distill in summer. Not because the process changes, but because the body changes: what it is processing, what it is holding, how its digestion is running, what the season is asking of it.

A single distillation gives you a single reading. A month of practice gives you a month of readings. A year gives you the range — and the range is where the pattern becomes visible.

What stays constant? What shifts with the moon? What shifts with the season? What correlates with sleep, with food, with emotional weather? A year of careful observation begins to answer these questions from the inside — not from a theory, but from the record your own body generates.

This is the Living Year as metric. Not a calendar to fill in. Not a programme to complete. A full rotation of the body through all its conditions, observed from inside the practice.

---

## The Monthly Rhythm

The body runs a complete alchemical cycle every month.

Menstruation is the *nigredo* — the blackening, the release, the dissolution of what was held. The follicular phase that follows is the *albedo* — the clearing, the brightening, the return of lightness and forward movement. Ovulation is the *citrinitas* — the golden moment, the peak of electrical charge and generative force. The luteal phase that follows is the *rubedo* — the reddening, the deepening, the integration of what the cycle produced.

Four stages. One month. Approximately thirteen times across a full year.

The quality of the material changes across this arc. What is distilled in the luteal phase has a different character from what is distilled in the follicular phase — denser, slower, heavier with what the body is processing. Neither is better. They are different fractions of the same ongoing work. Learning to notice the difference — to read the quality of what you are working with in relation to where you are in the cycle — is one of the refinements that only time teaches.

The lunar calendar and the menstrual cycle share the same period. This is not coincidence. The body's monthly alchemical sequence runs in attunement with the same rhythm that governs the aging of the material — the twenty-eight day hold mirrors the cycle that produced the material being held. The practice is already in conversation with the body's own timing. The year of observation makes that conversation legible.

## The Seasonal Character

Each season asks something different of the body, and the body responds.

**Winter** is the inward season. The body is consolidating, holding close, metabolising slowly. The material produced in deep winter tends toward density — heavier Saturn fraction, slower volatilisation, the solar fraction less immediately volatile than in warmer months. This is not a limitation. Winter material has a quality of depth, of the body having held what it processed for longer before releasing it. The aged winter preparation carries something that summer cannot produce.

**Spring** is the season of initiation. The body is waking up, the digestion quickening, the electrical charge of the system increasing as the light increases. The Aries-season distillate — late March through April — often shows the most vivid solar fraction of the year: bright, active, high-charge. The body has emerged from its winter consolidation and is moving. What it produces reflects that movement.

**Summer** is the season of the fully running system. Heat supports the practice — the external temperature assists the aging process, the body's own metabolic fire is running high. Summer material tends to be abundant and active, the fermentation faster, requiring closer attention and shorter aging windows in very hot weather. The practice runs hot in summer. The material knows it.

**Autumn** is the harvest and the turn. The body begins its inward movement, the light contracting, the system beginning to consolidate toward winter. Autumn material sits between summer's activity and winter's depth — transitional, rich, carrying the accumulated quality of a year of practice if you have been at it that long.

A year of practice gives you one complete seasonal rotation. The second year gives you comparison. By the third year you know your body's seasonal character — what it consistently produces in each quarter, what is specific to the season and what is specific to you.

## What Changes Across a Year

Some things that a year of careful observation tends to reveal:

**The diet becomes simpler.** Not through discipline but through legibility. The body begins to communicate clearly what it wants and what it does not — and the practice makes those signals harder to ignore. The appetite recalibrates. What was craving becomes preference. What was compulsion becomes choice.

**The material becomes cleaner.** As the dietary and hydration inputs become more precise, the prima materia becomes more refined. The distillate clarifies. The Saturn fraction becomes more structured. The year is itself a kind of rectification — repeated passes through the cycle, each one working the material a little further toward its essential character.

**The cycle becomes readable.** By the end of a year, most practitioners have a felt sense of where they are in the monthly arc — not from counting days but from the quality of what the body is producing and how they feel inside the practice. The cycle stops being something that happens to the body and starts being something the body does, with purpose, in its own time.

**The practice becomes ordinary.** This is the quiet transformation that a year produces. What was unfamiliar becomes familiar. The handling, the attention, the watching — it becomes the same kind of ordinary as tending a garden or keeping a kitchen. Present. Responsive. Not dramatic.

## How It Connects to The Living Year

The Living Year is the larger container.

The biophysics practice runs inside the body's own temporal rhythms — the lunar month, the seasonal quarter, the solar year. The Living Year is the framework that makes those rhythms visible and navigable. The lunar rhythm as the background field, always running. The solar year as the spine — light lengthening, light contracting, the equinoxes and solstices as natural thresholds. The living cycles as the intentional markings, the moments the year asks for attention.

The practice and the year are not separate systems. The practice is the most interior expression of the year — the body running its own alchemy in attunement with the rhythms the Living Year tracks. To do the practice with awareness of the seasonal and lunar context is to do it at a deeper register. The material you distill under the Scorpio full moon in autumn is being made by a body that the Living Year would describe as entering consolidation, processing the harvest, preparing for winter's inward turn. That context is not separate from the chemistry. It is the chemistry, in a language the practice can eventually begin to read.

The Living Year is the map. The practice is the territory.
