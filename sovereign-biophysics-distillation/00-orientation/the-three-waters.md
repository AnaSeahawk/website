# The Three Waters

The still made the one substance speak in three voices.

Before distillation, the material is whole. It carries everything together: the volatile, the clear, the fixed; the scent and the mineral, the charge and the residue, the part that wants to rise and the part that will not leave the chamber. The raw or aged fluid is one body.

Heat reveals the differences.

The first voice is the **Solar spirit**. It rises early. It is sharp, volatile, alkaline, immediate. The body recognises it by its speed: the smell, the sting, the way the mouth and skin know it has arrived before the mind has sorted the chemistry. In the measured record it is the fraction that most clearly carries the charge inversion. In the felt record it is the spark.

The second voice is the **Lunar essence**. It is the clear middle water, quieter and more stable. After the first volatile surge settles, this is what continues to come through: transparent, reflective, soft in its force. It became the daily drinking water in this practice because the body received it differently from ordinary water. Not as stimulation. As recognition.

The third voice is the **Saturn serum**. It does not rise. It remains in the chamber as the dense retentate: mineral, dark, earthy, weighted with what the water could not carry over. This was the fraction the practice had been approaching for years before the still arrived, through open evaporation and reduction. The still did not invent it. The still made its place in the whole sequence clear.

The names are alchemical because the operation is alchemical. They are also practical. Solar names the volatile rise. Lunar names the clear carrier. Saturn names the fixed body. Each name keeps a relationship visible.

The point is not that every run must be forced into three equal parts. Fresh material behaves differently from aged material. A first pass behaves differently from a redistillation. Sometimes the practice uses the whole distillate together. Sometimes the fractions are separated. Sometimes the retentate is absent or minimal because the starting material has not aged long enough to build the fixed body.

The point is that the material is not one inert thing.

It has registers.

The archive's work is learning how each register behaves: what the meters say, what the body recognises, what changes with diet, what changes with cycle, what changes with time in the jar, what changes when the same water passes through the still again. The fractions are not doctrine. They are a way of listening to the separation the material itself performs.

The Solar spirit asks for attention.

The Lunar essence asks for relationship.

The Saturn serum asks for patience.

Together they are the first translation the still gives: the body has been speaking in layers all along.
