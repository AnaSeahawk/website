# Where to Begin

---

This archive documents a practice, a body of research, and a way of reading experience without flattening it.

The practice is old — older than any written record of it, though the written records go back five thousand years and are surprisingly specific. It involves the body's own living waters: what the body produces, what those productions carry, and what becomes possible when a practitioner works with them directly rather than discarding them.

The short version: the body is a distillery. What moves through it is not waste. The traditions that understood this — Sumerian, Vedic, Egyptian, Alexandrian — left records in myth, in apparatus, in pharmacological systems still in use today. Those records were suppressed, burned, or reclassified as primitive. The knowledge survived in the body itself, because the body cannot be burned.

This archive is the record of one practitioner working her way back through that suppression — through the history, the biology, the lived practice, and the measurements — to document what the body already knows and what the tradition was always describing.

---

## How to Read This Archive

This archive contains several kinds of knowing.

Some documents are field records.
Some are measurements.
Some are working hypotheses.
Some are historical comparisons.
Some are symbolic or mythic readings.
Some are philosophical orientations.
Some are personal records from one body over time.

They are related, but they are not interchangeable.

A measurement is not a myth.
A myth is not a laboratory result.
A symbolic resonance is not automatically evidence.
A personal experience is not universal instruction.
A hypothesis is not a conclusion.

The layered structure of this archive emerged because lived experience did not arrive in isolated categories. Body, environment, symbolism, chemistry, perception, memory, ritual, weather, timing, emotion, and meaning often appeared together. Over time, the archive became too relational to remain coherent without learning to distinguish the different environments each observation belonged to.

The purpose of the [Dictionary](../Dictionary/README.md) and claim ecology is not to reduce the work into rigid systems. Their purpose is to preserve relationship without collapse.

Read for the layer that is active:

- what was observed;
- what was measured;
- what appears repeatedly;
- what is interpreted;
- what is hypothesized;
- what belongs to symbolic language;
- what belongs to philosophy;
- what remains unresolved.

Uncertainty is part of the integrity of the archive.

This work does not force every experience into institutional language. It also does not treat every sensation, symbol, or intuition as proof.

The archive is attempting another approach:

- measurements remain measurements;
- myths remain myths;
- observations remain observations;
- hypotheses remain provisional;
- philosophy remains declared;
- uncertainty remains visible.

The reader may encounter conductivity readings beside dreams, chemistry beside mythology, field notes beside historical analysis, symbolic language beside technical vocabulary, and bodily observation beside open questions.

This is intentional.

The observer is not removed from the field.

The observer is part of the field.

That does not remove the need for discernment. It increases it.

The archive asks neither for belief nor dismissal.

Only careful reading.

Slowly enough for each layer to remain what it is.

## What You Will Find Here

**The reading ethic** — claim environments, claim tiers, and language guardrails for moving through a mixed archive without confusing poetry, measurement, hypothesis, and proof.

**The lineage** — the women who carried this knowledge through five thousand years, what was done to what they knew, and why it matters that their names are still recoverable.

**The mechanism of erasure** — how a word replaces a question, and which specific words were used to close inquiry into the body's own productions.

**The mythological layer** — how the oldest recorded myth, the story of Inanna, is also a precise description of the alchemical process and the body's own chemistry.

**The material map** — fresh plasma, aged plasma, distillate, Solar, Lunar, Saturn, serum, retentate, blends, and reunited forms held in language that distinguishes substance, symbol, and interpretation.

**The empirical record** — one distillation run, measured with modern instruments, documented in full.

**A year of observation** — what the practice looks like across a complete solar cycle, and how it connects to the body's own seasonal and monthly rhythms.

**The living record** — the practitioner's account, protected where needed, because not every true record belongs in public view.

## Public and Private Layers

This public archive holds orientation, bounded method notes, measurements, analysis, synthesis, and vocabulary.

Some operational and intimate field records live in the private Vessel layer. That includes body application, mucosal pathways, touch, sexuality, partnered practice, and relational field observations.

Those records are not lesser data.

They are held privately because they require protection, consent, and context.

Public summaries may eventually point toward that layer in bounded language. The intimate operational details remain private unless explicitly approved.

## Who This Is For

Anyone who has felt something stirring in this territory and had no language for it.

Practitioners who are already in the work and want the historical and philosophical depth behind what they are doing.

Those who find the body's own intelligence more compelling than what institutions have said about it.

This is not a course. There is no curriculum, no programme, no outcome promised. It is a living archive — one practitioner's record, shared because careful knowledge gains value by moving with boundaries intact.

---

## Function

This archive functions as a living observational system.

Its purpose is to preserve relationships across time: between body, environment, ritual, perception, symbolism, measurement, temporality, and lived experience.

The taxonomy, Dictionary, and claim ecology emerged in response to the archive's own growth. As the work deepened, it became necessary to distinguish observation, interpretation, hypothesis, historical comparison, symbolic resonance, and philosophical orientation without severing the relationships between them.

The structure is still evolving.

The archive is learning to read itself.
