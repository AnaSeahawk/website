# A Related Body of Work

Olivier Francoeur — my partner, and the other practitioner documented in this archive's experiment records — has been building a parallel body of work from the classical Indian side of the same inquiry. Where this archive arrives at structured water through biophysics, his work arrives at *rasa* through Āyurveda. Where this archive reads sediment in glass jars, his work reads the same stratification through the lens of Vedic fermentation science. Different instruments. The same field.

His archive is called [The Book of Sol](https://github.com/LiGoldragon/TheBookOfSol). It is primary-source driven — Caraka Saṃhitā, Haṭha Yoga Pradīpikā, Ṛgveda, Damar Tantra — and written for readers who want the classical ground beneath the practice, not a summary of it.

This document is a one-way pointer. It names the points of overlap most relevant to the work in this archive, and links to the specific files in his body of work that speak most directly to what is documented here. No changes are required in his archive to use this as a reading companion.

---

## Where the Two Bodies of Work Converge

**The sediment protocol.** The deepest overlap. His [Plasma Recycling Manual](https://github.com/LiGoldragon/TheBookOfSol/blob/main/water/Plasma_Recycling_Manual.md) describes aging, stratification, and siphoning using the same three-tier model this archive documents visually in the sediment field guide: clarified living fraction rising, waste settling, volatile material distilling off. Different vocabulary for a procedure you can run with both texts open.

**Living water vs. geological water.** His [Keep the Plasma](https://github.com/LiGoldragon/TheBookOfSol/blob/main/water/Keep_the_Plasma.md) and [The Distilled Water Paradox](https://github.com/LiGoldragon/TheBookOfSol/blob/main/water/The_Distilled_Water_Paradox.md) make the Āyurvedic case for why the body's own processed fluid is more coherent than any external water source. This archive makes the biophysics case through structured water research. Read together they form a complete argument from two directions.

**Mineral intelligence.** His [Inorganic Minerals](https://github.com/LiGoldragon/TheBookOfSol/blob/main/chloride/Inorganic_Minerals.md) argues from classical Āyurveda that only biologically transformed minerals — plant-chelated, metabolically processed — can nourish living tissue. This archive approaches the same question from the soil ecology side. The gap in each work is what the other covers.

**The two pillars.** His [The Two Pillars of Nourishment](https://github.com/LiGoldragon/TheBookOfSol/blob/main/ayurveda/The_Two_Pillars_of_Nourishment.md) establishes the solar/lunar polarity — distilled plasma as the solar pillar, ghee as the lunar — that this archive's orientation section develops through the myth of Inanna and the circuit of the two distillates. The framing is different; the structure is the same.

**The closed loop.** His [Vajrolī](https://github.com/LiGoldragon/TheBookOfSol/blob/main/yoga-tantra/Vajrol%C4%AB.md) covers the three practices — Vajrolī, Sahajolī, Amarolī — as a single progressive principle: from deliberate restraint to closed-loop recycling of vital essence. This archive documents that loop from the outside through distillation; his work documents it from the inside through the yogic tradition.

---

The converging thesis, stated plainly in both bodies of work: a living body has its own intelligence, its own organizational field, its own capacity for self-regeneration — and that capacity is undermined precisely when external inorganic inputs are substituted for the body's own living processes.

His archive is the classical-lineage track. This archive is the contemporary-biophysics track. Neither is complete without the other.
