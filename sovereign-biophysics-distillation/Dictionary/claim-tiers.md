# Claim Tiers

Claim tiers describe the support behind a statement.

They are not publication status. They are a reading aid.

## tier_0_poetic

Symbol, image, devotional language, mythic phrasing, or aesthetic truth.

This tier may carry meaning, but it is not empirical evidence.

## tier_1_personal_observation

Single-subject observation from the practitioner's own body, environment, practice, or field record.

This tier may be precise and valuable. It remains local to the observed context.

## tier_2_measured_observation

Instrument reading or technical record with stated conditions.

This tier should name the instrument, sample state, limits of the measurement, and any missing baseline.

## tier_3_comparative

Pattern, resemblance, or convergence across traditions, texts, histories, symbolic systems, or repeated archive observations.

This tier can show relationship. It does not automatically prove causation or shared mechanism.

## tier_4_hypothesis

Provisional explanation that proposes how something may work.

This tier should remain revisable and should name what evidence would strengthen, weaken, or refine it.

## tier_5_documented_external_record

Historical, textual, biochemical, or scientific statement grounded in an external source.

This tier depends on citation quality, source reliability, and accurate framing.

## Mixed Claims

Many archive passages contain more than one tier.

When a statement crosses tiers, the transition should be visible. The reader should be able to tell when the archive moves from observation to interpretation, from measurement to hypothesis, or from symbolic resonance to comparative claim.
