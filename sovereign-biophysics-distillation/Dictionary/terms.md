# Terms

Working definitions for the key words that recur across the archive.

Each entry names the active registers where a word can operate and the guardrail that keeps those registers from collapsing into one another.

---

## Plasma

### Working Definition

*Plasma* is a working term for the body's living carrier fluid as it appears inside the practice, the field record, and the symbolic-philosophical language of Sovereign Biophysics.

It does not automatically mean blood plasma. It should not be read as a standard biomedical claim unless the context explicitly says so.

### Active Registers

- **Field:** the collected living substrate under observation
- **Instrument Table:** a sample with measurable properties such as pH, TDS, ORP, conductivity, scent, appearance, and fraction behaviour
- **Interpretation Chamber:** the body-produced carrier of charge, history, and condition
- **Mythic Field:** prima materia, living water, source substance
- **Philosophical Ground:** the body's own production treated as meaningful rather than discarded

### Guardrail

When the archive speaks in an external biomedical register, the term should be translated or qualified so it is not confused with blood plasma or presented as a universal scientific category.

## Distillate

### Working Definition

A *distillate* is the liquid that crosses through vaporization and condensation during a distillation run.

In this archive, the word may refer to a whole collected output or to a named fraction, depending on context.

### Active Registers

- **Instrument Table:** the condensate collected under stated conditions
- **Method:** a practical category for whole distillate, early fraction, middle fraction, late fraction, or repeated pass
- **Interpretation Chamber:** refined carrier fluid after separation from heavier material
- **Mythic Field:** risen and returned water, the flight and descent of the matter

### Distinctions

- **Whole distillate:** output collected as one continuous yield
- **Fraction:** output separated by timing, temperature behaviour, sensory shift, or measurement
- **Retentate or serum:** material remaining in the boiling chamber, not distillate

### Guardrail

The word *distillate* names process and material state. It does not, by itself, establish safety, effect, purity, or medical value.

## Field

### Working Definition

*Field* names the living context in which observation happens.

It may refer to direct experience, environment, relational atmosphere, or the archive's wider observational ecology.

### Active Registers

- **Field:** the immediate lived event
- **Pattern Room:** the context that repeats around an observation
- **Interpretation Chamber:** the relational setting that gives an event meaning
- **Philosophical Ground:** reality understood as relational rather than isolated

### Guardrail

The word *field* does not automatically mean electromagnetic field, biofield, or a measurable force.

If a technical field is meant, the measurement method or source should be named.

## Evidence

### Working Definition

*Evidence* is support for a statement.

In this archive, evidence can include observation, measurement, recurrence, external sources, comparative patterns, and symbolic resonance. These forms do not carry the same weight or answer the same kind of question.

### Forms

- **Direct observation:** what was experienced or recorded
- **Measured observation:** what an instrument registered under stated conditions
- **Repeated observation:** what returned across time or context
- **External record:** what a source, text, study, or historical document supports
- **Comparative pattern:** resemblance or convergence across systems
- **Symbolic resonance:** meaningful correspondence within mythic or philosophical language

### Guardrail

Symbolic resonance is not empirical proof.

Measurement is not total explanation.

Single-subject observation is not universal law.

Evidence becomes trustworthy when its kind is named.

## Hypothesis

### Working Definition

A *hypothesis* is a provisional explanation.

It proposes a possible relationship or mechanism without claiming finality.

### Role in the Archive

Hypothesis language belongs in the Hypothesis Forge. It may arise from field observation, measurement, repeated pattern, comparative archive work, or external research.

Its value is not certainty. Its value is that it gives future observation something clear to test, refine, or release.

### Guardrail

A hypothesis should remain visibly provisional.

It should not be written as proof, prescription, doctrine, or settled mechanism.
