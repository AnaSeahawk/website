# Distillate

## Working Definition

A *distillate* is the liquid that crosses through vaporization and condensation during a distillation run.

In this archive, the word may refer to a whole collected output or to a named fraction, depending on context.

## Active Registers

- **Instrument Table:** the condensate collected under stated conditions
- **Method:** a practical category for whole distillate, early fraction, middle fraction, late fraction, or repeated pass
- **Interpretation Chamber:** refined carrier fluid after separation from heavier material
- **Mythic Field:** risen and returned water, the flight and descent of the matter

## Distinctions

- **Whole distillate:** output collected as one continuous yield
- **Fraction:** output separated by timing, temperature behaviour, sensory shift, or measurement
- **Retentate or serum:** material remaining in the boiling chamber, not distillate

## Guardrail

The word *distillate* names process and material state. It does not, by itself, establish safety, effect, purity, or medical value.
