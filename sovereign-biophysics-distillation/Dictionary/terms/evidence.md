# Evidence

## Working Definition

*Evidence* is support for a statement.

In this archive, evidence can include observation, measurement, recurrence, external sources, comparative patterns, and symbolic resonance. These forms do not carry the same weight or answer the same kind of question.

## Forms

- **Direct observation:** what was experienced or recorded
- **Measured observation:** what an instrument registered under stated conditions
- **Repeated observation:** what returned across time or context
- **External record:** what a source, text, study, or historical document supports
- **Comparative pattern:** resemblance or convergence across systems
- **Symbolic resonance:** meaningful correspondence within mythic or philosophical language

## Guardrail

Symbolic resonance is not empirical proof.

Measurement is not total explanation.

Single-subject observation is not universal law.

Evidence becomes trustworthy when its kind is named.
