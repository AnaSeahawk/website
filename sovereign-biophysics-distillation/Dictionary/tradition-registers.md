# Tradition Registers

This page explains how traditional, symbolic, and comparative systems are used inside Sovereign Biophysics.

A tradition can orient the work without becoming proof for every claim.

## How To Read A Tradition Reference

When the archive names Ayurveda, qigong, neidan, alchemy, Buddhist medicine, mythology, or bioelectric research, ask:

- Is this a documented external source?
- Is this a comparison across systems?
- Is this a symbolic or mythic reading?
- Is this a practical vocabulary used inside the work?
- Is this a hypothesis about mechanism?

The same word can move across more than one register. The active register should remain visible.

## Register Table

| Register | What It Can Do | Boundary |
|:---|:---|:---|
| Documented source | show that a text, practice, apparatus, or concept exists in a tradition | does not prove the archive's interpretation by itself |
| Comparative map | place similar patterns beside each other | resemblance is not identity |
| Symbolic language | give image, rhythm, and meaning to what is observed | symbolic resonance is not empirical evidence |
| Practice vocabulary | name how a practitioner inside the work understands a process | internal vocabulary may need translation for public or scientific readers |
| Hypothesis | propose a possible mechanism across systems | remains revisable |

## Ayurveda

Ayurveda appears as a language for digestion, tissue formation, ojas, agni, anupana, soma, guna, and constitutional context.

Inside the archive, Ayurvedic language can clarify qualities: heavy/light, heating/cooling, unctuous/dry, mobile/stable, nourishing/depleting.

**Primary environments:** Comparative Archive, Interpretation Chamber, Philosophical Ground, Practice Ethic.

**Guardrail:** Ayurvedic language does not automatically make a biomedical claim. It gives a traditional qualitative map.

## Qigong, Chinese Medicine, and Neidan

Qigong and Chinese internal alchemy appear through qi, jing, shen, lower dantian, kidney water, jade broth, microcosmic orbit, triple burner, and inner furnace language.

Inside the archive, these systems help map refinement, circulation, essence, breath, heat, and the transformation of source material.

**Primary environments:** Comparative Archive, Hypothesis Forge, Interpretation Chamber, Practice Ethic.

**Guardrail:** Qi, jing, and shen are not automatically interchangeable with pH, ORP, hormones, or conductivity. Any bridge between traditional and biochemical language should be named as comparison or hypothesis.

## Alchemy

Alchemy appears through prima materia, solve et coagula, Tria Prima, Sulfur, Mercury, Salt, VITRIOL, athanor, tribikos, Chymical Wedding, and Opus Mulierum.

Inside the archive, alchemical language often works as a field guide for separation, volatility, fixation, return, vessel, heat, and timing.

**Primary environments:** Comparative Archive, Mythic Field, Interpretation Chamber, Philosophical Ground.

**Guardrail:** Alchemical language can describe process precisely without becoming a modern laboratory assay.

## Buddhist and Pali Sources

Buddhist sources appear especially where the archive discusses lifetime medicine, fermented source material, and the monastic medical record.

**Primary environments:** Comparative Archive, Interpretation Chamber, Practice Ethic.

**Claim tier:** direct source references belong to `tier_5_documented_external_record` when the source is cited clearly.

**Guardrail:** A textual record establishes that a passage or practice existed. The interpretive move connecting it to the archive should still be named.

## Mythic Field

Mythic language appears through Inanna, Ereshkigal, Prabhuta, Venus, the vessel, descent, return, and sacred story.

Inside the archive, myth is read as image, process, pattern, and human truth.

**Primary environments:** Mythic Field, Comparative Archive, Philosophical Ground.

**Guardrail:** Mythic truth is not the same kind of truth as instrument measurement.

## Bioelectric and Reichian Language

Bioelectric language appears through charge, polarity, conductivity, ORP, armoring, expansion, contraction, and surface potential.

Inside the archive, this language helps compare liquid measurements with organismic charge-state questions.

**Primary environments:** Instrument Table, Comparative Archive, Hypothesis Forge.

**Guardrail:** ORP measurements of a liquid are not the same measurement as Reich's skin-surface bioelectric readings. Parallels should be named as structural unless directly measured.
