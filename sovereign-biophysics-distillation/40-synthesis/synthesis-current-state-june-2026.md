# Current State of the Work — June 2026

June changed the phase of the archive.

The May synthesis held the work inside clearing: the dietary simplification, the
agni frame, the early fresh-versus-aged contrast, the question of what the
fractions were becoming after February's first measured recognition.

June brought the work into flow.

The shift reached beyond concept. It appeared in the household rhythm, the fresh
source material, the distillation records, the menstrual/artava threshold, the
movement practice, and the aged fractionation run. The archive now has enough
June records to name a new current state without closing the questions they
opened.

This remains one practitioner's record, one household, one still, and one living
context. No medical or therapeutic claim is made.

---

## I. What June Added

June added four kinds of record that May did not yet hold clearly.

First, the fresh-material record became more exact. The archive now has paired
fresh and distilled measurements from June 10, a five-batch household flow day
from June 17, and a same-day raw variation record from June 18.

Second, menstrual material entered the measured archive. The June 13 run
included menstrual blood, artava, in the source material. The cycle became part
of the measured distillation record, alongside symbol, timing, and lived body
knowledge.

Third, the practice became visibly household-scale. The June 17 entry documents
a daily field in which fresh plasma, distilled plasma, milk, food, skin,
drinking, cooking, aged material, and distillation were all moving through the
same cycle.

Fourth, aged fractionation produced a new repeatability signal. The June 20
aged-blend run gave two independent first-pass spirit readings from the same
aged bottle, plus a separately measured serum fraction.

Together, these records move the archive from a question about isolated runs
into a question about a living field.

The household has joined the still as part of the apparatus.

---

## II. The Fresh Distillate Signature

The June fresh-plasma records strengthened one of the archive's clearest
measurement patterns.

Fresh source material can vary widely. The distilled output repeatedly narrows.

On June 10, two fresh batches entered the still with very different
conductivity:

| Record | Fresh EC | Distilled EC | Distilled pH |
| :--- | ---: | ---: | ---: |
| June 10 Batch A | 4,160 uS/cm | 1,110 uS/cm | 9.6 |
| June 10 Batch B | 10,080 uS/cm | 1,120 uS/cm | 9.4 |

The starting material differed by more than twofold. The distillates landed
within a narrow band.

The same signature held across the June 17 full-cycle day. Five distilled
batches clustered around pH 9.4-9.5 while their measured conductivity and
hydrogen values moved more freely. The distilled material showed coherence even
when the fresh material and the daily selection process were not uniform.

Inside this archive, a working signature is visible:

- fresh source material is variable;
- distillation repeatedly produces an alkaline output;
- conductivity tends to drop strongly through distillation;
- pH is more stable than dissolved hydrogen;
- hydrogen and ORP remain condition-sensitive.

The stable feature is alkalinity.

The sensitive feature is charge expression.

---

## III. Source State Became A Primary Variable

June also made the source state harder to ignore.

On June 18, two fresh raw samples from the same body on the same day differed
strongly in dissolved load while pH barely moved.

| Sample | pH | EC | TDS | ORP | H2 |
| :--- | ---: | ---: | ---: | ---: | ---: |
| Early morning | 5.2 | 3,570 uS/cm | 1,800 ppm | +3 mV | 0 ppb |
| Midday fresh | 5.3 | 13,590 uS/cm | 6,850 ppm | +33 mV | 0 ppb |

The pH moved by 0.1.

Conductivity and TDS rose almost fourfold.

That record matters because it keeps the still from becoming too tidy. A
distillate can look stable while the source material entering the still is
changing across the day. The archive now has to track source timing, elapsed
time before measurement, hydration, digestion, temperature, selection, handling,
and household rhythm more carefully.

The source is a moving record, not a fixed input.

---

## IV. Artava Entered The Vessel

The June 13 menstrual run is a threshold record.

The raw morning material contained menstrual blood. It measured pH 5.1, EC
10,690 uS/cm, TDS 5,400 ppm, ORP +94 mV, and no measurable H2 before
distillation.

After the first distillation, the material shifted to pH 9.1, EC 2,490 uS/cm,
ORP -41 mV, and H2 84 ppb. After redistillation, it measured pH 9.7, EC 915
uS/cm, ORP -42 mV, and H2 88 ppb.

The familiar arc held:

- acidic raw source to alkaline distillate;
- high dissolved load to lower dissolved load;
- positive ORP to negative ORP;
- no measurable H2 to detectable H2.

What changed was the question.

The archive can now speak about cycle material through measurement as well as
metaphor, myth, endocrine timing, and lived body knowledge. Artava has entered
the measured field. One run marks the cycle as part of the experimental record.
It does not say what menstrual material changes.

That changes the responsibility of the archive.

The feminine fraction is in the jar.

## V. Full-Cycle Practice Became Visible

The June 17 record gave the archive a new scale.

The day was framed by circulation. Fresh plasma, aged plasma, distilled plasma,
food, milk, skin application, drinking, cooking, laundry, enemas, and
distillation were all present in the same household field.

The phrase that emerged from that record was "creating our own ocean."

That matters because the ocean moves through exchange, weather, evaporation,
return, depth, and tide. The household cycle began to feel less like a set of
separate applications and more like one moving water body with many expressions.

The measured data did not contradict the felt record. It clarified it.

Fresh samples varied. Distilled outputs clustered. Selection happened all day:
clearer material was often consumed directly, denser material was more likely
to enter the still. The measurements therefore belong to a lived cycle, not to
an isolated laboratory sequence.

This is the current methodological correction:

The record includes the run and the life that produced it.

## VI. Rasa And Qì Moved The Question From Substance To Participation

The June 19 rasa/qì entry names the felt turn that followed the full-cycle
shift.

The body was asking for movement, structure, attention, and participation.

This is a change from the April and May clearing phase. The earlier work was
shaped by removal: coffee, CBD, refined sugar, excess density, inherited noise,
and the old stabilizers the body had used to get through. June still carries
clearing, but the center of gravity has moved.

The body is now asking how nourishment moves.

Rasa becomes the key because it names fluid essence, nourishment, and the first
movement of what the body receives and makes its own. Qì becomes useful beside
it because it keeps the question relational: circulation, breath, movement,
contact, awareness, and the capacity of the parts to find one another again.

Rasa and qì remain different languages touching a shared field of experience.

In the June record, the material moves inside a field of participation: movement
on the floor, attention in tissue, household rhythm, diet, skin, mouth, gut,
bowel, breath, and the slow return of places that had gone quiet.

The body is participating in its own return.

## VII. Aged Material Showed A Different Register

The June 20 aged-blend run changed the fractionation question.

This record has a firm boundary: the source material was a minimum one-month-aged
blend from Ana and her partner. It cannot be read as one person's physiology. It
can be read as a household aged-source and apparatus record.

Within that boundary, the repeatability is strong.

Two independent first-pass distillations from the same aged bottle produced
closely matched spirit cuts:

| Fraction | Run A | Run B |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Spirit pH | 10.0 | 9.9 |
| Spirit EC | 16.83 mS/cm | 15.07 mS/cm |
| Spirit ORP | -94 mV | -97 mV |
| Spirit H2 | 190 ppb | 198 ppb |

The essence readings also clustered in ORP and H2:

| Fraction | Run A | Run B |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Essence pH | 9.3 | 9.7 |
| Essence ORP | -58 mV | -58 mV |
| Essence H2 | 124 ppb | 118 ppb |

The serum, captured only in Run B, marked the other pole: pH 6.1, EC 26.0
mS/cm, salinity 12.9 ppt, ORP +45 mV, and no measurable H2.

This is the clearest June image of the three waters as measured fractions:

- spirit as high-alkaline, high-conductivity, strongly reducing, hydrogen-rich;
- essence as quieter, lower-conductivity, still reducing, still carrying H2;
- serum as dense, conductive, more acidic, oxidizing, and without measurable H2.

One serum measurement gives the archive an edge to return to.

The spirit repeated. The serum marked the boundary.

## VIII. What The Archive Can Say Now

The archive can now say these things with more confidence than it could in May:

**Fresh distillation has a recognizable signature.** Across June 4, June 10,
June 13, and June 17, distilled fresh material repeatedly landed in an alkaline
range even when the source material varied.

**Source state matters.** June 18 showed strong same-day variation in raw
material. Distillation records need source timing and measurement timing
wherever possible.

**H2 is condition-sensitive.** Dissolved hydrogen moved widely across June. It
appeared after some distillations, decreased in one redistillation, held through
another, varied with jar timing on June 4, and repeated closely in the aged
spirit cuts on June 20.

**ORP needs more than a single distillation story.** Some raw samples entered
positive and became negative after distillation. Some fresh June 17 samples were
already negative before the still. Aged serum measured positive. The charge
story is real, and it is complex.

**Menstrual/artava material is now part of the measured archive.** The June 13
run opened a cycle-material line that needs repeated records before it can be
interpreted.

**Aged material behaves as its own source state.** The June 20 run belongs
outside the same category as fresh same-day plasma. Aging, storage, filtration,
syphoning, blending, and fraction timing all matter.

**The practice has moved from isolated experiment to living field.** The
household cycle, movement practice, community witness, and daily use pattern are
now part of the evidence context.

The work is becoming less abstract.

It is also becoming harder to simplify.

That is a good sign.

## IX. What Remains Open

The June record sharpened the open questions.

**Timing.** How much variation belongs to time of day, and how much belongs to
elapsed time before measurement?

**Fresh versus aged.** How many distinct source states are now in play: fresh,
same-day blended, aged, syphoned aged, filtered aged, menstrual fresh, blended
household aged?

**Hydrogen.** What controls H2 most strongly: temperature, time after
distillation, headspace, source state, pass number, density, meter timing, or
fraction identity?

**ORP.** When does distillation create charge inversion, when does it soften an
already negative source, and when does the fixed serum pole hold the positive
edge?

**Serum.** Does the June 20 serum profile repeat across aged bottles, stopping
points, source states, and collection methods?

**Artava.** What changes when menstrual material is present, and what belongs
only to morning concentration, hydration, or handling?

**The living field.** How should the archive record movement, rasa, qì, tissue
softening, and household flow without turning them into claims the measurements
do not yet support?

The next good records are not larger claims.

They are cleaner witnesses.

---

## X. The June Current State

The June current state is full-cycle observation.

The archive is documenting what one body and one household reveal when the whole
cycle is allowed to move: source, food, milk, movement, menstrual timing, stored
material, fresh material, distillate, serum, skin, mouth, gut, bowel, dream,
community, and the still.

The May work asked what had been clarified by clearing.

The June work asks what begins to move once the clearing has opened enough
space.

The answer is a field:

- fresh plasma varies;
- distilled plasma coheres;
- cycle material changes the question;
- aged material opens the three-water register;
- movement and attention change the felt body;
- the household becomes part of the method;
- the record must stay wide enough to hold the life around the measurements.

That is the new state of the work.

The archive is learning to read flow.
