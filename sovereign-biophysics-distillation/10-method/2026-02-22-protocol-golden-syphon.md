# The Amaroli Ritual of the Golden Syphon

**Date of Execution:** Sunday, February 22, 2026 (Sun Day / Taurus Moon)  
**Batch Origin:** February 1, 2026 (Sun in Aquarius / Moon in Leo)  
**Record Type:** Personal ritual log (single-subject, non-instructional)

Boundary note: this page documents one practitioner's ritual context and is not a protocol for others.

### I. The Internal Alignment (The Resonance)
The ritual begins not in the flask, but in the body. In this personal log, internal preparation was used as a resonance step with the same batch lineage. The intent was to align internal and external states before the run.

### II. The Gathering of *Tejas* (The Solar Incubation)
The chosen fluid is brought into the light. For two days, it was placed in direct sunlight as a solar incubation step. Through the night, it was kept warm before returning to morning sun exposure.

### III. The Separation of Worlds (The Yin-Yang Syphon)
The vessel was elevated and left undisturbed to allow gravity-based separation. In this framing, heavier sediment settles while the upper layer clears. The upper layer was then gently siphoned for the next phase.

### IV. The Ancestral Battery (The Golden Anchor)
The clarified fluid is placed back in the sun, seated upon an altar of ancestral alchemy. Beneath the vessel rests:
* **The Master’s Gold:** A handmade Yellowknife gold bracelet, gifted at 19, carrying the inscribed lineage of a father who mastered both the chemistry of the pharmacist and the noble metals of the goldsmith.
* **The Solar Stone:** A citrine teardrop held in a gold basket on a silver chain, anchoring the light.
* **The Sovereign Wealth:** A 20-Franc gold rooster coin.

In this personal symbolic framework, these items serve as an intentional anchor for lineage, abundance, and craft memory during the run.

### V. The Intention and the Heat (The Distillation)
After night stillness, morning alignment, siphon clarification, and symbolic anchoring, heat was applied and distillation began. Measurements and outcomes are logged in the corresponding experiment record.
