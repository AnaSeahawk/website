# Plasma Forms Taxonomy
## A Practical Map of Source, Fraction, Retentate, and Reunion

The practice uses one word, *plasma*, across several material states.

Those states are related, but they are not interchangeable.

This taxonomy names the forms so the reader can tell what kind of material is being described before the archive moves into interpretation, symbol, application, or mechanism.

This is a language map, not a protocol. It records how the terms are used inside this archive.

---

## Reading Boundary

The word *plasma* is a working term in Sovereign Biophysics. It names the body's living carrier fluid as it appears in this practice record.

It does not automatically mean blood plasma. It does not carry a medical claim by itself. When the archive uses a term in a biochemical, symbolic, operational, or philosophical register, the active layer should be visible.

For the claim environments behind this approach, see the [Dictionary](../Dictionary/README.md).

## Source Forms

### Fresh Plasma

Fresh plasma is the unaged source material.

It is the starting point before fermentation, aging, or distillation changes the material. Its character varies with time of day, hydration, diet, cycle phase, sleep, practice state, and the body's immediate condition.

Within the archive, the pre-dawn first void is treated as a reference form because it carries the longest overnight concentration window. This does not make every other fresh sample irrelevant. It gives the archive a stable comparison point.

**Claim environment:** Field, Instrument Table, Pattern Room.

### Aged Plasma

Aged plasma is fresh plasma held across time.

Fermentation and slow putrefaction alter scent, alkalinity, sediment, surface phenomena, and distillation behavior. In this archive, aged plasma is often stronger as a source for retentate and fraction separation than fresh plasma.

The aging period belongs to the material record. It should be named when known.

**Claim environment:** Field, Pattern Room, Hypothesis Forge.

## Distilled Forms

### Pure Plasma

Pure plasma is the whole clear distillate collected as one continuous output from a run, without separating the early, middle, and late fractions.

It is usually alkaline, optically clear, and much lower in dissolved solids than the source material. It carries the full distilled range as one combined form.

Pure plasma is useful as a general term only when the run was not separated into named fractions.

**Claim environment:** Instrument Table, Field.

### Spirit / Solar

Spirit is the earliest volatile fraction.

Solar is its energetic descriptor.

This fraction is light, sharp, active, and usually the most chemically expressive of the clear distillates. In the archive it is associated with higher alkalinity, volatile compounds, surface-tension shifts, scent changes, and the first crossing of the matter.

In alchemical language, this form corresponds most closely to Sulfur as volatile fire. That correspondence is symbolic and operational, not a claim that the material is elemental sulfur.

**Primary names:** Spirit, Solar.

**Alchemical register:** Sulfur.

**Claim environment:** Instrument Table, Pattern Room, Comparative Archive.

### Essence / Lunar

Essence is the middle clear fraction.

Lunar is its energetic descriptor.

This is the quieter carrier fraction: stable, clear, and less volatile than the Solar edge. It is the middle water of the run, the form that carries continuity after the first sharp rise has passed.

In alchemical language, this form corresponds most closely to Mercury as the messenger and medium. That correspondence names behavior: carrying, connecting, mediating.

**Primary names:** Essence, Lunar.

**Alchemical register:** Mercury.

**Claim environment:** Instrument Table, Field, Comparative Archive.

### Essence Tails

Essence Tails are the late liquid distillate that crosses as the chamber approaches the end of the run.

They belong to the border between Essence and Serum. They have crossed the condenser and landed in the receiver, but they carry more of the mineral character of what remains behind.

They are not the same as Serum. Serum never crosses.

**Primary names:** Essence Tails, late distillate.

**Claim environment:** Instrument Table, Pattern Room.

### Serum / Saturn

Serum is the dense retentate left in the chamber.

Saturn is its energetic descriptor.

This form did not rise. It is concentrated, mineral-rich, darker, heavier, and more materially specific to the source. In the archive it is where steroid conjugates, fixed minerals, dense organics, and heat-mediated transformation are discussed.

In alchemical language, this form corresponds most closely to Salt as the fixed body of the work.

**Primary names:** Serum, Saturn.

**Alchemical register:** Salt.

**Claim environment:** Instrument Table, Analysis, Hypothesis Forge.

## Post-Distillation Forms

### Blended Fractions

Blended fractions are practical combinations made after separation.

Examples include Lunar Essence carrying a small amount of Saturn Serum, or a whole output used without separation. A blend should be named as a blend so it is not confused with a single fraction.

**Claim environment:** Field, Practice Ethic.

### Reunited Fractions

Reunited fractions are the return movement after separation.

In alchemical language, this is *Coagula*: the restoration of what was separated. The public archive can describe the concept and the material categories. Detailed intimate application records belong in The Vessel.

**Claim environment:** Comparative Archive, Mythic Field, Practice Ethic.

### Mineral Salts

Mineral salts are the purified fixed matter obtained when the remaining earth of the process is further worked.

In the alchemical register this is the movement toward *Sal Salis*, the Salt of Salts. In the public archive, this term remains mostly symbolic and methodological until specific records name the material, process, and measurements.

**Claim environment:** Hypothesis Forge, Comparative Archive.

### Body Preparations

Body preparations are forms made for contact with the body, such as a serum reduction, balm, diluted wash, or carrier blend.

Public-facing descriptions remain bounded. Records involving mucosal pathways, touch, sexuality, arousal, partnered practice, or intimate relational field data belong in:

`Components/the-vessel/25-intimate-field-private/`

**Claim environment:** Field, Practice Ethic.

## Evidence Posture

The taxonomy keeps several layers apart:

- **Measured:** pH, ORP, TDS, conductivity, temperature, volume, visual state.
- **Observed:** scent, texture, surface behavior, sediment, clarity, body response.
- **Interpreted:** Solar, Lunar, Saturn, Spirit, Essence, Serum.
- **Compared:** Sulfur, Mercury, Salt, Nigredo, Albedo, Rubedo, Chymical Wedding.
- **Hypothesized:** constituent behavior, mechanisms, physiological significance.
- **Protected:** intimate application and relational field records.

The forms can speak to one another without becoming the same claim.

---

## Naming Convention

Use the working names when describing material:

- Spirit / Solar
- Essence / Lunar
- Serum / Saturn

Use the alchemical names when describing symbolic or comparative structure:

- Sulfur
- Mercury
- Salt

Use the biochemical language only when the claim is being made in the Instrument Table or Analysis layer.

The word should tell the reader which layer they are in.
