# Analysis: What the Scientists Found About Water

*A bridge between two bodies of external research and what the distillation runs are measuring.*

---

The runs produce numbers that are easy to record and harder to interpret. Two bodies of independent scientific research — one on structured water, one on plasma-activated water — help explain what is happening at the molecular level when the fractions separate.

---

## Structured Water: The Body's Internal Battery

In 2013, Dr. Gerald Pollack at the University of Washington published *The Fourth Phase of Water*, documenting that water near hydrophilic surfaces — including biological membranes — spontaneously organises into a gel-like state with a different molecular structure: not H₂O but H₃O₂, arranged in a hexagonal lattice.

This structured zone carries a stable negative charge. The bulk water beyond it carries a positive charge. The separation creates an electrical potential — water behaving as a biological battery, generating charge differential without any external input.

The fuel for building this structure is infrared light. Pollack's lab showed the structured zone expands immediately upon IR exposure — which is sunlight, which is what the solar activation step in the preparation protocol uses.

This matters to the runs because: the body is largely water next to hydrophilic surfaces. The internal environment is already producing structured, negatively charged water as a baseline biological state. What the distillation captures and concentrates — the negative ORP of the Lunar fraction, the charge differential between fractions — may be reflecting something the body is generating continuously at the cellular level, made visible at a measurable scale.

---

## Plasma-Activated Water: Electricity Changes Water's Chemistry

In June 2015, researchers at Notre Dame led by David Go published findings in *Nature Communications*: direct optical evidence of electrons from plasma dissolving into water. These solvated electrons don't penetrate deeply — they are captured within the top 2.5 nanometers of the surface — but their effect is fundamental. Plasma does not just heat water. It alters the electron surface chemistry, turning the water into a reactive, electron-donating medium.

This is relevant because the Solar fraction — the volatile first cut of an aged distillation — presents with strong negative ORP (electron-donating, reducing), high alkalinity, and high conductivity. The conventional explanation is ammonia chemistry: urea breaks down to ammonia during aging, ammonia vaporises first under heat, condenses into ammonium hydroxide, and produces the alkaline spike. That is documented biochemistry and it is correct.

But the plasma research adds a second lens: the aged biological fluid, before distillation, has been living in a charged biological environment for weeks. The electron chemistry of the fluid at the point of distillation is not neutral. What comes over in the first fraction may carry more of that charge than the bulk water that follows.

---

## The Connection

Neither body of research was conducted with endogenous distillation in mind. Both were studying water itself — its structure, its responses to electrical charge, its behaviour near surfaces and under light.

What they collectively describe is a medium far more active and responsive than simple solvent chemistry assumes. Water organises. It holds charge. It responds to light. It changes register when plasma passes through it.

The runs measure these properties directly: ORP, conductivity, pH across fractions. The numbers are not exotic. They are measurements of what water is doing under these specific conditions — and the external research helps explain why those numbers look the way they do.
