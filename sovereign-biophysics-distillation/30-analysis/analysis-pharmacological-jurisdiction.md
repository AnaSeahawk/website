# What the Model Was Built For
## On the Architecture of Pharmaceutical Medicine

---

*Facts in this piece are drawn from the published and public record. The structural argument — that the pharmaceutical model was oriented toward commercial dependency rather than health — is interpretation, offered as such.*

**Source-proximity note:** This document uses public-record and biomedical sources for dates, laws, trial findings, and epidemiological estimates. The interpretation that links those records into a dependency architecture belongs to this archive. It is not presented as medical, legal, or historical consensus.

## I. What Gets Called Biology

Before a drug reaches a human body, it is tested on cells in dishes.

Not living cells in context — extracted cells, cultured in artificial media, maintained under laboratory conditions that share almost nothing with the conditions of a living body. Most of the cell lines used in pharmaceutical research are immortalised: cells that escaped the normal regulatory mechanisms of life in order to survive outside it. They survive by being aberrant. The behaviour that allowed them to survive the extraction is the behaviour that made them no longer normal cells.

This is the foundation of the science. Not a living body. Not even a living fragment. A dead fragment, under artificial conditions, studied as a proxy for life.

Bark stripped from a tree, placed in a dish, studied under controlled conditions. The findings applied to the tree as if the bark were the tree.

The bark tells you something. It tells you about the surface chemistry at the moment of stripping. It tells you nothing about the sap, the root system, the mycorrhizal network the tree is in conversation with, the way the tree responds to drought or season or the specific community of organisms it has grown among for decades. Remove the bark from all of that and you have removed it from everything that makes it what it is in a living system. What you are studying is no longer a tree. It is a fragment of a tree, in a context that has never existed in nature, responding to stimuli under conditions that will never recur in a forest.

A drug that produces effect X in an immortalised cancer cell line may produce nothing of the kind in a living female body cycling through its monthly intelligence, embedded in a nervous system, in conversation with a microbiome, regulated by a hormonal cascade the cell in the dish does not have and cannot replicate. The cell in the dish is not a model of the body. It is a controlled experimental object that shares some chemistry with the body.

The research proceeds as if these are the same thing.

## II. The Model That Made This Seem Rational

The reason the petri dish was ever considered an adequate research site is a specific philosophical event with a specific history.

For over a thousand years, European medicine operated within the Galenic humoral framework — a model of health as dynamic balance among four humors in continuous relationship with environment. The body was understood as porous, relational, in exchange with everything around it: food, air, season, emotional state. Not a mechanism but an ecology. Health was not the absence of pathology but the maintenance of balance in a living, responsive system.

René Descartes' *Traité de l'homme* — written around 1633, published posthumously in 1662 — replaced this with the body as machine. Digestion, circulation, muscle movement, nerve function: mechanical operations. The body is an extremely sophisticated mechanism, but it is a mechanism. Its operations are in principle entirely explicable by physics. Descartes himself preserved the rational soul as exempt from this — the mind was not the machine. La Mettrie's *L'Homme Machine* (1748) removed even this exception. The human being, fully mechanical, all the way through.

This was not an isolated philosophical claim. It arrived alongside Harvey's mechanical pump model of the heart (1628), Newton's mechanistic physics, and the entire apparatus of the scientific revolution. By the eighteenth century, the Galenic understanding of the body as a relational ecology had been substantially replaced by the Cartesian understanding of the body as a mechanism with parts.

What the machine model made possible: the parts can be separated. The part in isolation operates by the same mechanical laws as the part in the system. The findings from the part scale to the whole because the whole is just parts in arrangement.

What it made invisible: the body's intelligence. Context. Relationship. Temporal variation. The fact that a cell in a body is in continuous communication — with the hormonal system, the nervous system, the immune system, the microbiome, the organ it's embedded in — and that removing it from all of that communication does not produce a simpler version of what it does in context. It produces something different.

A machine has no intrinsic intelligence. A body is nothing but intelligence. The machine model could not account for this, so it classified it out of existence. Intelligence became epiphenomenon. Variation became noise. The body's own signals became the pathology to be suppressed.

Once the body is a mechanism, the petri dish is a valid research site. Once the body is an intelligent, relational system, it is not. The entire pharmaceutical research infrastructure rests on the prior philosophical move that made it seem sufficient.

## III. The Naming as Commercial Architecture

The naming events that structured pharmaceutical medicine followed a consistent pattern.

Gerardus Mulder isolated a class of compounds from blood, egg albumin, and serum albumin in 1838. The Swedish chemist Berzelius, writing to Mulder in July of that year, suggested the name "protein" — from the Greek *proteios*: primary, standing in front. The material being studied: extracted animal tissue. Isolated. Described by elemental composition.

Ernest Starling introduced "hormone" in his Croonian Lectures to the Royal College of Physicians in June 1905. The discovery it followed: acid introduced into the denervated duodenum of an anaesthetised dog, the intestinal mucosa scraped and filtered, the filtrate injected intravenously — the pancreas secreted. A chemical messenger travelling through blood, not nerves. Named from the Greek *hormon*: I arouse to activity.

Casimir Funk proposed "vitamine" in 1912 — vital amine — from his work on the anti-beriberi factor isolated from rice polishings: the discarded outer layers of processed rice whose absence caused deficiency disease. The name was already partially wrong: not all vitamins are amines. The "e" was dropped in 1920.

Each naming event followed the same sequence: extract a compound from biological material, measure its effects under controlled conditions, assign a name, establish a category. The category becomes a thing. The thing becomes a product. The product enters the market.

The market requires the body to be understood as deficient in the named thing so that the named thing can be supplied from outside. The vitamin becomes a tablet. The hormone becomes a pharmaceutical. The protein becomes a supplement.

But these names describe patterns recognised in extracted, isolated material. What a protein *is* in a living body is a dynamic process: folding, signalling, responding, being produced and broken down and recycled in a continuous loop of cellular intelligence that the dish cannot contain. The name describes what could be measured from a dead fragment. The product sells back to the living body what the name captured from its dead one.

The naming was the infrastructure. The deficiency model followed from it. The body cannot be adequate to itself within this framework — because adequacy would eliminate the market.

## IV. The Pipeline

The male-body default is not a policy introduced at the level of human trials. It is structural from the earliest stages of research.

A 2001 study of neuroscience publications found that the sex of cell lines was reported in fewer than 25% of papers. When reported, male cells dominated. The omission was not flagged as a methodological problem. The male cell was not understood as a male cell. It was understood as a cell — the default, the standard, the universal case.

Male cell line. Male animal model. Predominantly male clinical trial. Universal human prescription.

The female body enters the development chain nowhere. By the time a woman takes a medication, every layer of the knowledge it was built on — mechanism of action, receptor behaviour, metabolic pathway, dose calibration — was mapped on a different biological system.

The rationale given at each stage was consistent: the female hormonal cycle introduced variability. Variability is a confounding variable. Confounding variables compromise the data. The solution: remove the variable.

What was removed was not an inconvenience to the science. It was the primary variable of interest for half the population who would eventually take the drug. The complexity was not a problem with the female body. It was a problem with a research model that could only process uniformity, because it had already decided the body was a mechanism and mechanisms should produce consistent output under consistent conditions.

The FDA formalised this in 1977, recommending the exclusion of women of childbearing potential from early clinical trials. The FDA's 1993 gender-differences guidance explicitly names that earlier 1977 "Women of Childbearing Potential" section as the policy it was revising. An act of Congress — the NIH Revitalization Act of 1993 — was required to mandate the inclusion of women and minorities in NIH-supported clinical research. The NIH issued a further directive in 2014 requiring sex to be considered as a biological variable even in preclinical cell-line and animal research. Twenty-one years after Congress mandated women in trials, the NIH was still instructing researchers to stop using exclusively male cells.

**Source note:** FDA 1993 notice revising the 1977 exclusion language: [NIH Guide NOT93-224](https://grants.nih.gov/grants/guide/notice-files/not93-224.html). NIH inclusion mandate: [NIH policy on women and minorities](https://www.grants.nih.gov/policy-and-compliance/policy-topics/inclusion/women-and-minorities/guideline). NIH sex-as-biological-variable policy development: [NOT-OD-14-128](https://grants.nih.gov/grants/guide/notice-files/NOT-OD-14-128.html).

The exclusion was not reversed. It was formally extended to earlier stages of the pipeline twenty years after the clinical trial correction was legislated.

## V. What the Concentration Removes

The path from plant to pharmaceutical follows the same logic as the path from body to petri dish: isolate the active component, remove the context, increase the concentration, measure the effect.

A plant carries its own modulators. Across millions of years of proximity — plant secondary metabolites and animal receptor systems in continuous relationship — what the plant offers arrives with compounds that slow absorption, counteract extremes, buffer the effect, signal the body toward saturation. The phytocannabinoid-terpenoid research documents this precisely: whole cannabis preparations produce different effects from isolated THC at equivalent doses, because the isolated compound does not carry the modulators the whole plant does (Russo, *British Journal of Pharmacology*, 2011). The broader natural products literature documents repeatedly that "activity is lost upon fractionation" — the extract does not behave as the whole thing behaved. The body has an evolutionary relationship with the whole thing. It does not have an evolutionary relationship with the isolated alkaloid at pharmaceutical concentration.

The endocannabinoid system — named after cannabis because research on THC revealed it — exists in the mammalian body because endogenous ligands for those receptors evolved independently. The plant and the animal body developed alongside each other over geological time. The receptor system was already there; the plant chemistry fit it because the fit is co-evolutionary, not accidental.

The pharmaceutical compound has no such relationship with the body. It arrives as a stranger, at concentrations nothing in the body's evolutionary history prepared it for.

The threshold between therapeutic and lethal in a pharmaceutical compound is one measure of how far the compound has been removed from relationship. A common culinary herb such as chamomile is not usually discussed through the same narrow therapeutic-index frame as a concentrated pharmaceutical isolate, though plant medicines can still carry contraindications, allergies, and interactions. The isolated compound, concentrated toward the white-powder endpoint of the purity logic, has had much of the surrounding context removed. In this archive's reading, that purity is also the removal of intelligence the context carried.

Stronger does not mean better. It means more removed from living relationship, and therefore less able to be read correctly by the living body receiving it.

## VI. The Cascade

You go to the doctor on a Tuesday.

The appointment is fifteen minutes. You report how you feel that day — a snapshot, one moment in the body's ongoing intelligence. The context is not available to the appointment: the phase of your cycle, the sleep deficit you've been carrying, what you've been trying to process, the nutritional state your body is managing, what would happen if you simply had three days of rest. The appointment does not have instruments for any of this. It has instruments for the measurable symptom presented.

The snapshot becomes the diagnosis. The diagnosis produces the prescription. The prescription addresses the measured symptom, not the body's underlying communication.

The body — an intelligent system that responds to everything — responds to the drug. The drug's effects produce new signals. The new signals become new symptoms. The new symptoms become new prescriptions. Each prescription produces its own effects, which produce their own symptoms, which produce further prescriptions.

Among American adults aged 60–79, 34.5% take five or more prescription drugs simultaneously. Across older U.S. adults more broadly, polypharmacy has nearly doubled in twenty years: from 23.5% in 1999–2000 to 44.1% in 2017–2018. Women are prescribed medications at higher rates than men, and multiple adverse-drug-reaction studies report higher ADR rates in women. In a recent older-adult oncology cohort, polypharmacy and clinically significant drug-drug interactions were each associated with substantially higher ADR odds after adjustment; the exact risk size is population-specific, but the direction of risk is not marginal.

**Source note:** U.S. polypharmacy trend: [NHANES 1999-2018 analysis](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/37434230/). Sex differences in ADR-related admissions and ADR risk: [Eur J Clin Pharmacol 2021](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC8091530/) and [Biol Sex Differ 2020](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC7275616/). Polypharmacy/DDI adjusted risk example: [Front Pharmacol 2025](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC12158949/).

The original signal — whatever the body was trying to say on that Tuesday — is buried under layers of pharmaceutical response. The body was communicating something. That communication has been interrupted, layer by layer, until neither the person nor their doctor can locate what the original message was. The question the model is not designed to ask: *symptom of what?* Not what measurable pattern matches a named category. What was the intelligent system attempting to move through before it was interrupted?

The body produces pain as a signal that something requires attention. It produces inflammation as a repair process. It produces low mood as a processing state. Each of these is the body doing something — moving toward resolution, attempting to complete something, communicating what it needs. The pharmaceutical model names these processes as the pathology and suppresses them. The message is silenced. Whatever the body was trying to say remains unsaid. The resolution the body was working toward does not complete.

The body was not malfunctioning. It was talking. The model could not listen, so it muted.

## VII. The Diagnosis as Installation

The harm does not require a drug. The encounter itself is an intervention.

Receiving a diagnosis is not a neutral information event. It is a naming delivered by a figure of institutional authority, in a context of maximum vulnerability, with high emotional charge. The conditions under which suggestion is accepted without evaluation — authority, repetition, emotional arousal, suspended critical faculty — are precisely the conditions of the clinical diagnosis encounter. The doctor says *you have X*. The body receives an instruction. Not because the patient is passive or gullible: because the body is an intelligent system that responds to the narrative it inhabits.

The nocebo effect is measurable. In Ted Kaptchuk's open-label placebo trial at Harvard (*PLOS ONE*, 2010), patients with irritable bowel syndrome told explicitly they were receiving inert sugar pills reported 59% adequate relief — higher than typical double-blind placebo response rates in pharmaceutical IBS trials. Reviews of nocebo research also document that clinical disclosure can shape symptom expectation and reported adverse effects. The naming of the possible effect can produce a proportion of the effect.

**Source note:** Open-label placebo trial: [Kaptchuk et al., PLOS ONE 2010](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC3008733/). Nocebo and informed consent review: [Colloca and Miller, 2011](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/21862825/).

The diagnostic error literature adds a further dimension. Ductal carcinoma in situ — DCIS — is routinely treated as breast cancer. DCIS research does not support one clean progression percentage across all grades and contexts, but it does document substantial overdiagnosis and overtreatment concern, especially for lower-risk lesions. The treatment for diagnosed DCIS is commonly surgery, often followed by radiation and sometimes endocrine therapy. Applied to a body that would never have developed invasive disease, the harm is manufactured by the diagnostic encounter and the treatment it drove.

**Source note:** DCIS progression and overtreatment are active research questions, not settled by a single percentage. Useful anchors: [Finding the balance between over- and under-treatment of DCIS](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/27671693/) and [Low-risk DCIS review](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC8983540/).

The thyroid cancer case is the clearest documentation. Between 1993 and 2011 in South Korea, thyroid cancer diagnosis rates increased fifteenfold after the introduction of ultrasound screening capable of detecting small nodules. Mortality was completely flat throughout the entire period. After public awareness campaigns about overdiagnosis, diagnosis rates fell substantially. Mortality did not change — in either direction. The improved instrument found more things to name. The naming drove treatment. Treatment carries harm. The underlying danger it was treating did not exist at the scale the diagnosis rate implied.

**Source note:** South Korea thyroid screening/overdiagnosis: [Ahn, Kim, and Welch, NEJM 2014](https://www.nejm.org/doi/full/10.1056/NEJMp1409841).

The diagnosis installs a reality. The body enacts the reality it has been given. The original state — whatever the body was moving through before the encounter — may have been temporary, cyclical, in the process of resolving. The diagnosis reframes it as a condition to be managed indefinitely. The person who might have recovered becomes the person who has a thing they have.

## VIII. The Protection That Made All of This Possible

In any other industry, producing documented mass harm through products known to carry significant risk, suppressing unfavourable data, and continuing to operate after evidence of widespread injury would constitute a criminal matter.

The pharmaceutical and medical sector operates within a legal and regulatory architecture specifically designed to prevent this outcome.

The **1986 National Childhood Vaccine Injury Act** redirected vaccine injury claims into a federal no-fault compensation program, removing the standard civil litigation pathway. The Supreme Court's 2011 ruling in *Bruesewitz v. Wyeth* extended this protection to preempt design-defect claims entirely. Manufacturers retain liability for manufacturing defects and deliberate fraud; the pathway for civil suit on the grounds that the product was designed in a way that caused harm was substantially closed.

**Source note:** *Bruesewitz v. Wyeth LLC*, 562 U.S. 223 (2011): [Justia Supreme Court archive](https://supreme.justia.com/cases/federal/us/562/223/).

The **PREP Act** provides blanket liability immunity to manufacturers, distributors, and administrators of medical countermeasures during declared public health emergencies. The declaration is the trigger. The immunity is automatic. It does not require demonstration that precautions were taken or harm was unavoidable.

**Source note:** HHS PREP Act description and Q&A: [ASPR PREP Act](https://aspr.hhs.gov/legal/PREPact/Pages/default.aspx) and [PREP Act Questions and Answers](https://aspr.hhs.gov/legal/PREPact/Pages/PREP-Act-Question-and-Answers.aspx).

**FDA user fee funding.** Under the Prescription Drug User Fee Act (enacted 1992), pharmaceutical companies pay fees to the FDA to fund the drug review process. In the current period, those fees cover 77% of the costs of the FDA's drug review program — the program that determines whether a drug reaches the market. The regulatory body that assesses safety and efficacy for pharmaceutical approval derives three-quarters of its operational budget for that assessment from the companies whose drugs it is assessing. This structural conflict of interest is documented in peer-reviewed literature (PMC8917050, 2022), not only in advocacy sources.

**Source note:** PDUFA authority: [FDA PDUFA overview](https://www.fda.gov/prescription-drug-user-fee-act-pdufa). FY 2024 user-fee obligations and non-user-fee appropriations: [FDA FY 2024 PDUFA Financial Report](https://www.fda.gov/media/185331/download). Conflict-of-interest discussion: [J Law Med Ethics 2022](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC8917050/).

The **standard of care defence**. A physician who followed accepted medical practice is protected from liability even when that practice caused harm. Protection scales with consensus: the more widely a practice is adopted, the more protected each individual practitioner who follows it. This produces a structural incentive toward conformity — toward prescribing what the guidelines recommend, where those guidelines are shaped by the clinical trial record, where that record is funded and designed by industry.

**Source note:** This is legal-structure interpretation built on malpractice doctrine. The baseline doctrine is that professional negligence is assessed against the applicable standard of care; see Cornell Wex on [standard of care](https://www.law.cornell.edu/wex/standard_of_care) and [malpractice](https://www.law.cornell.edu/wex/malpractice).

**Settlement as the cost of doing business.** Rofecoxib (Vioxx, Merck) was approved by the FDA in 1999 and withdrawn in September 2004 after evidence of increased cardiovascular risk became indefensible. FDA safety officer David Graham testified to Congress that Vioxx was likely responsible for at least 55,000 deaths; a 2005 *Lancet* analysis estimated 88,000 to 140,000 excess serious coronary heart disease cases in the United States, with an estimated 44% of those cases fatal. Merck's peak annual revenue from Vioxx exceeded $2.5 billion (2003). The 2007 settlement — $4.85 billion — resolved approximately 45,000 personal injury lawsuits. No Merck executive was criminally prosecuted. The relationship between projected liability and projected revenue is a calculation that can be performed before a drug ships.

**Source note:** Trial evidence of cardiovascular risk: [APPROVe trial, NEJM 2005](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/15713943/). Population estimate anchor: [Graham et al., Lancet 2005](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S0140673605178647), summarized in [BMJ 2005](https://www.bmj.com/content/330/7485/212.2).

The protection architecture was not assembled in response to unforeseen harm. It was built in advance of harm that was, in the actuarial sense, foreseeable. The harm is legally managed rather than prevented. This is not a corruption of a system otherwise oriented toward health. It is the system operating as designed — at the intersection of commercial interests with sufficient legal insulation to survive the consequences.

## IX. What This Work Is Doing in Relation to This

The Sovereign Biophysics archive documents the female body's endogenous chemical intelligence: what it produces, how those productions are structured by the hormonal cycle, what the distillation process reveals and separates and makes available to work with.

The practice treats the body's cyclical variability as primary intelligence — the thing to attend to, orient around, and follow. Not to control for. Not to manage. To read.

The pharmaceutical model classified exactly this intelligence as a confounding variable and built its research infrastructure around a body that did not have it. Multiple adverse-drug-reaction studies document higher ADR risk in women, though the exact size varies by population, medication class, endpoint, and study design. In the archive's reading, that pattern belongs beside the pipeline history that mapped the body in male cells, male animals, male trials, and then arrived at the female body as if it were a smaller version of the same instrument.

What was removed from the research record because it complicated the experimental design is what makes the female body the superior laboratory for this kind of work. The hormone-responsive, cycle-structured, endogenously producing body is not a deficient version of the male body. It is a different instrument with different capacities, different intelligences, and a different relationship to time. The practice documented in this archive is built on taking those capacities seriously — on treating what the pharmaceutical model excluded as exactly the material worth studying.

The question the pharmaceutical model was designed to prevent: *what does the body's own intelligence actually produce, and what becomes possible when that intelligence is worked with rather than overridden?*

The practice is an answer in progress.
