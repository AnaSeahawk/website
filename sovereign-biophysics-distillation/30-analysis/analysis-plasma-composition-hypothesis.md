# Hypothesising What Is in the Plasma

## Composition across the raw material, the aged fraction, and the three distillate streams

*A working hypothesis, not a claim. This document attempts to reason from what is well-established in biochemistry toward what the fractions likely contain — and to name clearly where the evidence ends and interpretation begins.*

---

## What We Can Say With Confidence

The core composition of urine is extensively documented. The classical reference is Putnam (1971), *Composition and Concentrative Properties of Human Urine* (NASA CR-1802), which tables exact solute concentrations across large populations. Roughly 95% is water. The remaining 5% is dissolved solids: urea (the largest fraction by mass), followed by electrolytes — potassium, sodium, chloride, phosphate, sulfate — then organic acids, creatinine, uric acid, and trace minerals including calcium and magnesium.

Every value in that table is an average over a mixed population eating a mixed diet. It is a starting point, not a description of any particular person's plasma.

What shifts the numbers is diet. This is also well-established. Remer and Manz (1995) characterised how dietary composition determines urinary acid load and pH. The nephrology literature on 24-hour urine analysis treats urinary electrolyte ratios as direct reflections of dietary intake — because that is what they are. Sodium in urine came from sodium in food. Chloride in urine came from chloride in food — primarily as NaCl. The ionic form (Cl⁻) is not itself metabolised into another compound and is excreted as chloride.

Chloride's effect on digestion is worth naming precisely, because it is often framed as a health requirement. It is not a requirement for healthy digestion — its presence affects digestion by expanding capacity for *high food volume*. Dietary chloride stimulates salivary secretion and activates salivary amylase; it feeds directly into the parietal cells' synthesis of hydrochloric acid. This chain — more chloride, more saliva, more gastric acid — is what makes it possible for a person to eat the quantities of cooked, concentrated food a standard diet involves. Remove the chloride and that capacity contracts. This is not a failure of health; it is the body returning to a digestive mode appropriate to a lighter load.

A further chemical reaction occurs in the body's tissues when chloride is present: it combines with hydrogen peroxide in neutrophils to form hypochlorous acid (HOCl) — the same chemistry as bleach. This is not a function the body is performing; it is a detrimental reaction happening to the body's own tissue. HOCl is chemically indiscriminate — it destroys cell membranes without distinction. The cascade from this reaction is broad and cumulative: chronic inflammation, tissue stiffness, water retention, cognitive fog, accelerated ageing of the skin and connective tissue. The quantity the average person consumes as a matter of routine — if taken all at once — would likely be acutely fatal. The body cannot keep up with excreting it; the excess is stored in tissue. This is not a substance where less is better. None is the correct amount. The fact that its effects are so widely distributed across the body is precisely why they are so rarely attributed to it — the connection is not made because the substance is considered normal. What appears in urine as chloride is what the body managed to clear; on a zero-NaCl baseline, even that remainder approaches zero. Potassium in urine reflects potassium intake. Sulfate reflects dietary sulfur (meat, eggs, alliums). Phosphate and calcium reflect mineral-dense plant foods and — crucially — bone metabolism under conditions of low mineral dietary load.

The practitioner baseline described in these records is specific and requires precise description, because several of the inputs are the fractions themselves re-entering the system:

**Fluids:**
- Primary drinking fluid: distilled plasma (shivambu). Not tap water, not mineral water — the practitioner's own distilled biological fluid, consumed daily.
- Distilled tap water (observed to behave and appear distinct from ordinary water — structured or deuterium-depleted character, see *Analysis: What the Scientists Found About Water*): used primarily for washing and cooking; occasionally consumed directly but not as the primary drinking fluid.
- Solar spirit (volatile distillate fraction): consumed orally, used for oral hygiene (teeth cleaning, mouthwash), used to wash grains, vegetables, and fruits before consumption, and applied topically.
- Lunar essence: consumed orally, applied liberally to the skin, introduced into cooking on occasion (particularly with ayurvedic medicinals). The primary topical fraction.

This is a closed loop. The fractions produced by distillation re-enter the body through multiple routes. The plasma the body produces tomorrow is downstream of the plasma consumed today.

**Diet:**
- Dairy present: warm milk, yogurt, ghee.
- Grain present: wheat and rice — washed with distilled water and Solar spirit, soaked before cooking. Oats are not soaked or washed in this way.
- Fruit and vegetables: occasional and intentional, not daily — used medicinally (fresh pressed juice or cooked apple to cool digestion or support transition off sugar).
- No meat, no eggs, no alliums, zero NaCl.

**Practice timeline and staged dietary changes:**
The amaroli practice is four years old as of April 3, 2026. The dietary baseline described here was not arrived at all at once — it represents the current state of a staged, long-term process:

**Transition state (active at time of writing, April 2026):**
The body is mid-transition through the removal of coffee, CBD, and refined sugar. This is not a stable equilibrium baseline — the plasma is being produced by a body actively clearing these substances, and the compounds being mobilised during that clearing will be present in the raw material. What the plasma carries during a clearing phase differs from what it carries at long-term stable baseline. Both are worth documenting; they should not be conflated.

*Practitioner observations during this transition are recorded separately in `The-Living-Year/journal/2026-04-23-the-deepest-layer.md`.*

This baseline shifts the plasma profile predictably in the following directions:

- **Sodium chloride**: absent. Zero NaCl in the diet — including distilled water used for all cooking to eliminate incidental sources — means NaCl is not present in meaningful quantity in the residue. NaCl is the largest single mineral load in a standard diet; its complete removal collapses the dominant electrolyte.
- **Potassium**: proportionally elevated. The body continues excreting electrolyte waste; with Na absent, K takes a larger share.
- **Phosphate and calcium**: relatively elevated. Dairy is a natural source of both; what passes into urine reflects dietary calcium load and mineral turnover.
- **Sulfate**: moderate. Dairy proteins (casein, whey) contain sulfur-bearing amino acids — methionine and cysteine — so the sulfate load is not negligible, but substantially lower than a meat-eating baseline. Ghee contributes almost no protein, so it has minimal effect on sulfate.
- **Urea**: present. Dairy protein turnover generates urea continuously; the load is lower than a meat-eating diet but not absent.

This is the composition of the raw material. It is a reasonable inference, not a measured value — without 24-hour urine analysis, exact concentrations are unknown.

## What Aging Does to the Raw Material

Urea hydrolysis by urease-producing bacteria is basic biochemistry (any microbiology textbook will cover it). In the presence of microbial activity, urea breaks down:

```
(NH₂)₂CO + H₂O → CO₂ + 2NH₃
```

Ammonia raises pH. Higher pH shifts the equilibrium of several other dissolved compounds. Some organic acids and amino acid derivatives become more stable; others become more volatile.

Proteins — the large-molecule component that makes fresh plasma difficult to work with — are physically digested by enzymatic activity during the aging period. Intact proteins resist vaporisation and create what the run logs describe as "thin gravy weirdness" in the fresh-material retentate. Fermented material does not behave this way because the heavy proteins have been broken down into smaller, water-soluble fractions: organic salts, amino acid derivatives, short peptides.

Allantoin — a metabolite of uric acid oxidation — is reported to increase with aging, an observation noted in the alchemical literature and consistent with what is known about uric acid oxidation under alkaline conditions.

The aged plasma is therefore not simply diluted or concentrated raw plasma. It is a chemically distinct substance: the mineral salts are now freely accessible rather than locked in protein matrices, the pH is elevated, and the volatile fraction has been enriched or altered by fermentation.

## The Three Fractions After Distillation

Distillation separates the aged plasma by volatility. Three fractions emerge.

### The Solar Spirit (first distillate, earliest volatiles)

What rises first under low heat is determined by boiling points. Water vapour (100°C) carries with it trace amounts of the most volatile dissolved compounds. In the context of an alkaline, fermented starting material, the most likely volatile co-distillates are:

- **Ammonia** (NH₃, bp −33°C) — highly volatile, very water-soluble. In the alkaline aged material, free ammonia is present. It will enter the distillate, particularly in early fractions.
- **Short-chain volatile organic acids** — acetic acid and related fermentation products, if present.
- **Indole, skatole, and other nitrogen heterocycles** — trace volatile metabolites of amino acid catabolism. Identified in urine distillate studies.

The Solar fraction is the most volatile and arguably the least mineral-rich. In alchemical framing, it is the spirit — that which the material could not hold. Biochemically, it is the portion most altered by fermentation and most dominated by volatile nitrogen compounds.

The alkalinity of the Solar fraction (measured at pH 9.9–10.09) has a documented effect on grain prepared with it: wheat, rice, and oats soaked in distilled water with Solar spirit added break apart more readily during cooking than grain soaked in water alone, with longer soaking producing better results. The most likely mechanism is alkaline hydrolysis of hemicellulose and structural proteins in the grain — the same principle as nixtamalization, where an alkaline soak (traditionally lime or wood ash) structurally transforms corn. Ammonia present in the Solar fraction would contribute to this effect.

The use of distilled water as the soaking medium appears to amplify the Spirit's effect. Tap and bottled water contain calcium, magnesium, chlorine, and other dissolved ions that compete with and buffer the Spirit's alkaline compounds. In distilled water — very low TDS, few competing ions — the Spirit's active compounds act directly on the grain without interference. A cleaner solvent allows the active compounds more unimpeded contact. Distilled water alone also cleans grain surfaces more effectively than tap or bottled water, consistent with the behaviour of low-TDS water, which has greater capacity to pull soluble compounds off surfaces.

These are practitioner observations, not controlled comparisons, but each is consistent with known chemistry.

**What is uncertain:** exact volatile composition without gas chromatography. The character of the Solar fraction will vary substantially with fermentation duration and starting material.

### The Lunar Water (bulk aqueous distillate)

The Lunar fraction is predominantly water — that is the point of distillation. The question is what it carries.

Pure water distillate would carry nothing. But water distilled from a complex biological matrix carries:

- **Trace ammonia** (highly water-soluble)
- **Low-molecular-weight organic compounds** that can steam-distil or partition into the vapour phase
- **No fixed salts** — sodium, potassium, phosphate, calcium, and sulfate have boiling points well above anything reached in a simple still; they remain behind

The Lunar water is mineral-depleted but not completely inert. The documented low ORP (oxidation-reduction potential) and slight alkalinity suggest it is carrying something that affects its electrochemical character — most likely dissolved ammonia from the early ferment, or other reduced nitrogen species.

The Ayurvedic tradition's characterisation of this fraction as having a distinct energetic quality — "unctuous," having passed through a living system — is a qualitative observation. What it corresponds to biochemically is an open question.

### The Saturn Retentate (what remains in the chamber)

Everything the distillation could not lift into vapour is here. On the dietary baseline described, this means:

- **Potassium salts**: potassium phosphate, potassium sulfate, potassium citrate, and related compounds. These are the dominant fixed salts when NaCl is completely absent from the diet. Potassium phosphate and potassium citrate are hygroscopic — they absorb water from the air rather than desiccating the skin. This is the property that makes the retentate workable as a topical preparation.
- **Calcium phosphate**: present in trace amounts. The mineral of bone and tooth enamel.
- **Magnesium salts**: present. Magnesium is a cofactor in hundreds of enzymatic processes; it appears in urine at consistent concentrations regardless of diet.
- **Uric acid derivatives**: the less volatile breakdown products of purine metabolism.
- **Amino acid residues and short peptides**: what survived the fermentation digest without becoming volatile. These may include compounds relevant to skin — the literature on natural moisturising factor (NMF) in skin identifies amino acids, pyrrolidone carboxylic acid (PCA), and urocanic acid as components.
- **Creatinine**: a stable metabolic waste product, largely inert.

What is *absent* (on this dietary baseline): sodium chloride in significant quantity. The absence is as important as the presence. NaCl crystallises aggressively at room temperature and is osmotically desiccating; its absence is what makes the retentate smooth rather than sharp-edged.

## What This Framework Cannot Tell Us

The above is reasoning from population-level biochemistry applied to an inferred dietary baseline. It does not constitute measurement. The gaps are real:

1. **We do not have a 24-hour urinalysis** for the practitioner. Exact starting concentrations are unknown.
2. **The water input is uncharacterised beyond its observable properties.** The distilled water used for all cooking and washing has distinct visual clarity and behaviour consistent with structured or deuterium-depleted water, but has not been tested. If it is meaningfully deuterium-depleted or carries a negative ORP baseline (see Pollack), it is itself an active variable in what enters the body — and therefore in what the plasma carries. This is not a minor footnote; the water is a primary input.
3. **We do not have GC/MS data** on the Solar or Lunar fractions. What volatile compounds are present, and at what concentrations, is inferred not measured.
4. **The aging process is not controlled or characterised** in terms of microbial community, pH trajectory, or fermentation endpoint. Duration is tracked; chemistry is not.
5. **The interaction effects between fractions are unknown.** Whether the Lunar water carries something biologically active beyond its electrochemical measurements is an open question.
6. **The alchemical and Ayurvedic interpretive layer is not resolved by this analysis.** The claim that the aged, distilled material has distinct properties as *a practice substance* — beyond what biochemistry can currently describe — belongs in a separate register. This document does not confirm or refute that claim.

The electrochemical measurements documented in this body of work — fresh baseline (pH 7.0, ORP +77 mV), aged raw baseline (pH 9.1, ORP -22 mV), and the three distilled fractions — are the available bridge between the biochemical hypothesis and the observed material. They characterise the electrical and ionic state of each fraction without requiring resolution of the interpretive frame.
