# Fresh vs Aged: Comparative Distillation Behaviour

## The Two Laws

### 1. The 2-Pass vs 3-Pass Law

Fresh and aged inputs follow mechanically distinct distillation pathways.

**Fresh plasma (two-pass):** Fresh biological fluid (< 24 hours old) contains large, intact protein structures that resist vaporisation. The primary challenge is thermal hydrolysis of intact urea — the urea must be driven to degrade into CO₂ and volatile ammonia before the water cleanly separates. This happens across two passes: Phase 1 initiates the hydrolysis; Phase 2 flashes off the ammonia produced in Phase 1 and yields the pure water body. Fresh fluid reliably reaches the ~135 USCM stable range in two passes.

**Aged plasma (three-pass):** Gestation functions as enzymatic pre-digestion — the microbial activity during aging breaks down intact proteins and converts them into water-soluble organic salts and dissolved ions. This increases the conductive load of the starting material. The result is that Phase II distillate of aged material remains highly conductive (~993 USCM) after two passes and requires a third polishing run to reach the ~133 USCM stable range. Aging creates more dissolved content that must be progressively refined out.

The divergence is not a deficiency in either pathway — it reflects what each starting material is. Fresh plasma is structurally dense and protein-intact; the challenge is hydrolysis. Aged plasma is pre-digested and mineral-rich; the challenge is conductivity reduction.

### 2. The Law of the Saturn Yield — The 1/4 Volume Protocol

The practical divergence in retentate behaviour is one of the most consequential observations in the archive.

**Fresh retentate:** As the water is stripped from fresh plasma, the intact macro-proteins condense into a structurally unstable sludge — dense, unworkable, what the run logs call "thin gravy." The fresh earth is biologically locked. The 1/4 volume protocol cannot be executed on fresh material; the residue is not retained.

**Aged retentate (Saturn Gold):** The fermentation process physically dismantles the protein matrix before distillation begins, converting it into stable, water-soluble organic salts and minerals. This means that when the run is terminated at one-quarter of the original volume remaining in the chamber, what remains is a workable concentrate — dense, hygroscopic on a zero-NaCl dietary baseline, suitable for topical preparation and the Coagula phase of the practice.

The 1/4 volume protocol applies to aged input only. The aging is not simply a step in the process — it is the condition that makes the Saturn portion accessible at all.

## Three Contrast Baselines — Open Experimental Threads

Three significant batches are now waiting, each produced under a distinct dietary baseline, each representing a different chapter of the practice. When distilled and compared, they will produce the archive's first direct baseline comparison series.

**Torremolinos, Spain.** Approximately 60 litres in three 20-litre containers (Olivier's, Ana's, combined), produced during the Torremolinos period — zero sodium chloride, low sulfur, with daily coffee and non-psychoactive CBD as part of the baseline architecture. Both coffee and CBD were discontinued before departure from Spain and have not resumed. This batch carries the signature of that specific period: cleaner than the Beloeil material, but distinct from the post-coffee, post-CBD baseline that follows. Return to Spain is planned. That run series will produce a direct before/after comparison — same practitioner, same storage location, measurably reduced volatile load.

**Beloeil, Quebec.** A deeply aged batch gestating since before the Torremolinos period, produced at an earlier stage of the practice — before the dietary baseline reached its current refinement. Extended aging and the earlier baseline make it a distinct contrast: same zero-sodium foundation, different dietary stage, different geography and season.

**Current baseline (post-Spain).** Coffee and CBD discontinued before leaving Spain, maintained since. Any runs from this point forward represent the cleanest dietary baseline in the archive to date — lower volatile potential, reduced stimulant metabolites, continued zero sodium chloride and low sulfur. All Sarandë-period distillation runs from fresh material represent this baseline.

All three are open experimental threads, not conclusions.

## Working Observations

*Single-subject. Not generalisable without further comparison.*

1. **Near-neutral ORP event (0 mV).** One triple-distilled fraction reached 0 mV at 133 USCM — a near-neutral electrochemical state, neither electron-donating nor electron-receiving. Treated here as a notable extraction-state marker, not a universal threshold.

2. **Gestation and retentate behaviour.** Fresh runs consistently produced unstable residue; aged runs consistently produced a denser, workable retentate. The pattern is stable across multiple runs in this single-subject record and supports further comparison of gestation windows.

3. **Dietary baseline as process variable.** Diet functions as an upstream electrical variable affecting volatility, foam behaviour, and retentate profile. This is being carried forward as a testable control-vs-baseline question across the three contrast batches above.
