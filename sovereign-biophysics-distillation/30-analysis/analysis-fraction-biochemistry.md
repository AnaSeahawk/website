## 1. The Form of Hormones in Urine Before Distillation

Understanding what distillation does to hormones requires first understanding what form they take in urine. The assumption that hormones exist in urine as their free, biologically active forms is incorrect. The liver conjugates steroid hormones prior to renal excretion, rendering them water-soluble but significantly reducing their direct receptor activity.

### 1.1 Conjugation: The Primary Mechanism

Steroid hormones and their metabolites appear in urine almost exclusively as water-soluble conjugates — glucuronides and sulfates. Compounds with a 3β-hydroxyl group are excreted primarily as sulfates, while 3α-hydroxysteroids are excreted as glucuronide conjugates. This conjugation is mediated by hepatic glucuronosyltransferases and sulfotransferases.

### 1.2 Testosterone in Urine

Approximately 50% of testosterone is metabolised via conjugation to testosterone glucuronide and, to a lesser extent, testosterone sulfate. A further 40% is metabolised into the 17-ketosteroids androsterone and etiocholanolone through the actions of 5α- and 5β-reductases, 3α-hydroxysteroid dehydrogenase, and 17β-HSD. These metabolites are subsequently glucuronidated and excreted. Free (unconjugated) testosterone in urine represents only a minor fraction of total urinary androgenic content.

### 1.3 Estrogens in Urine

The principal urinary estrogens are conjugates of estriol and 2-hydroxyestrone. Estrogen metabolism proceeds in the liver and kidney through conjugation of estriol, epiestriols, and catechol estrogens to glucuronides and sulfates — forms that are highly water-soluble and excreted rapidly. Although conjugated estrogens have lower direct biological activity than free estrogens, they are capable of being cleaved back to free forms under appropriate conditions.

### 1.4 DHEA and Upstream Steroids

DHEA (dehydroepiandrosterone) and its sulfate (DHEA-S) are present in substantial quantities in urine. DHEA serves as the primary precursor to both testosterone and estrogen synthesis and is itself biologically active. Pregnenolone metabolites, upstream of the entire steroid cascade, are also present. The complete steroid precursor chain — cholesterol → pregnenolone → progesterone → DHEA → androstenedione → testosterone / estradiol — leaves metabolic signatures across the urinary steroid profile.

## 2. What Happens During Distillation

### 2.1 Volatility and Non-Volatility of Steroids

Steroid hormones are large, non-volatile molecules with very high boiling points. Estradiol, for example, would require temperatures exceeding 220°C at near-vacuum pressure to distil — conditions far beyond simple water distillation. In their conjugated forms they are even more firmly non-volatile. The practical consequence is clear: steroid hormones do not meaningfully travel into the distillate fraction. Research using direct contact membrane distillation of urine has demonstrated greater than 99.5% retention of estrone and 17β-estradiol — confirming that hormones concentrate in the residual fraction, not the distillate.

### 2.2 Heat-Mediated Deconjugation in the Retentate

A critical secondary process occurs during distillation: the application of heat begins to cleave glucuronide bonds between the steroid and its conjugate group. Enzymatic hydrolysis of urinary steroid glucuronides occurs at 55–60°C with incubation times of one to two hours — temperatures and durations entirely consistent with distillation practice. Non-enzymatic acid/base hydrolysis is also accelerated by heat. This means that as distillation proceeds, some fraction of conjugated hormones in the retentate is progressively cleaved toward their free, unconjugated, biologically active forms.

The duration and temperature of the distillation process therefore directly influences the ratio of conjugated to free hormones in the retentate. Longer, lower-temperature reduction may preserve more intact conjugates alongside gradually increasing free hormone fractions. **The retentate is not a static concentrate but a chemically evolving preparation.**

## 3. Full Constituent Catalogue by Fraction

### 3.1 Spirit — The Solar Distillate

*Ayurvedic character: Laghu (light), Ruksha (dry), Ushna (warm). The risen fraction. Comparable to the volatile, purified, pranically active dimension of the original substance.*

- **Acetone and ketone bodies** — particularly present with vegetarian or low-carbohydrate dietary patterns

The Spirit fraction is closest to structured biological water carrying light volatile information. Its purity and lightness make it suited to internal use and the most accessible entry point for practitioners new to the practice. Its documented alkalinity (pH 9.9–10.09) is consistent with the ammonia content and produces measurable alkaline hydrolysis effects on grain soaked in it — discussed in *[The Living Grain →](../10-method/the-living-grain.md)*.

### 3.3 Serum — The Saturn Retentate

**Minerals (concentrated):**

## 4. Transdermal Absorption of Steroid Hormones

The relevance of the Saturn retentate as a topical preparation rests on the established fact that steroid hormones cross the skin barrier efficiently. This is not a marginal or speculative claim — transdermal delivery is a primary pharmaceutical route for both estradiol and testosterone, used clinically because it:

- Bypasses first-pass hepatic metabolism, delivering hormones directly into systemic circulation
- Provides steady, sustained absorption compared to oral routes
- Allows dose titration through application area and frequency
- Is effective at physiological concentrations

Estradiol transdermal patches, gels, and sprays are standard menopausal hormone therapy precisely because the molecule crosses skin reliably. Testosterone gels are standard hypogonadism treatment in men for the same reason. The same molecular properties that make pharmaceutical transdermal delivery work apply to the steroid content of the Saturn retentate.

The distinction between pharmaceutical preparations and the retentate is concentration precision and conjugation state — not mechanism. The retentate contains a complex mixture of conjugated and, with heat-mediated deconjugation, partially free steroids, delivered in a biological matrix alongside growth factors, minerals, and peptide signals. The pharmaceutical analogy establishes the plausibility of the mechanism; the complexity of the mixture is the retentate's own character.

## 5. The Significance of EGF in the Retentate

Epidermal Growth Factor (EGF) deserves specific attention as a constituent of the Saturn retentate. EGF is a 53-amino-acid peptide that binds the EGF receptor (EGFR) and stimulates:

- Keratinocyte proliferation — accelerating skin cell renewal
- Fibroblast activation — driving collagen and elastin synthesis
- Wound healing and tissue repair
- Modulation of melanocyte activity — relevant to skin tone evenness

EGF is present in urine and concentrates in the retentate. Applied topically in concentrated form, it represents a plausible biochemical mechanism for observed improvements in skin tone, elasticity, and sun tolerance that are independent of — though complementary to — the hormonal effects. High-end cosmetic research is actively pursuing urine-derived and synthetic EGF as a skin rejuvenation agent.

The practitioner record documents changes to skin tone, elasticity, and sun tolerance across three years of daily topical serum application. The EGF mechanism, alongside transdermal steroid delivery, provides two distinct and biochemically established explanations for these observations. They do not need to compete; both are likely operative.

## 7. Dietary and Environmental Modification of Fraction Composition

The composition of all three fractions is not fixed — it reflects the practitioner's current biology, which is itself shaped by diet, environment, cycle phase, and practice history. This is not a limitation but a feature: the preparation is a living biological mirror.

### 7.1 Vegetarian Diet

Vegetarian dietary patterns alter urinary steroid excretion profiles. Soluble dietary fibre modifies bile acid excretion and affects the colonic beta-glucuronidase activity that regulates estrogen enterohepatic recirculation. Vegetarian practitioners generally show altered estrogen metabolism patterns, including differential excretion ratios of estrone, estradiol, and estriol conjugates. The zero-sodium-chloride dietary practice further shifts mineral and electrolyte composition of the retentate — documented in *[Hypothesising What Is in the Plasma →](analysis-plasma-composition-hypothesis.md)*.

### 7.2 Cycle Phase

In cycling women, urinary hormone profiles shift dramatically across the menstrual cycle. Follicular phase urine contains rising estrogen conjugates; luteal phase urine is dominated by progesterone metabolites (pregnanediol glucuronide) alongside sustained estrogen. The retentate produced across a full cycle therefore contains a composite hormonal signature of the entire cyclical pattern.

### 7.3 Partner Contribution

In shared-practice contexts where both partners contribute to a combined distillation, the fraction composition reflects both individuals' hormonal profiles simultaneously. Male urinary androgen output is substantially higher than female output — testosterone metabolite concentrations in male urine are significantly greater. The combined retentate therefore contains both estrogenic and androgenic hormonal material, with each partner receiving back a preparation incorporating the other's endocrine signature transdermally.

## 8. Storage Considerations

The stability of retentate constituents over time depends on storage conditions. Key variables are temperature, light exposure, container material, and duration.

- **Temperature**: Cold storage significantly slows degradation of peptide constituents (EGF, growth factors, LH/FSH fragments) and reduces leaching from plastic containers. Refrigeration is preferred; dark cool storage is the practical minimum.
- **Light**: UV exposure degrades steroid hormones and peptides. Dark storage is essential.
- **Container material**: Hard HDPE (recycle symbol 2) and polypropylene (5) are the most stable common plastics for biological material storage. Steroid hormones show some adsorption to plastic surfaces over time, and plasticiser leaching (BPA, phthalates) introduces xenoestrogen contamination into stored material. Transfer to amber glass is recommended for long-term storage where possible.
- **Peptide stability**: Growth factors and gonadotropin fragments are the most labile constituents. Significant degradation is expected over months at ambient temperature; cold storage slows but does not eliminate this.
- **Steroid conjugates**: Relatively stable under cold dark conditions. Four months cold storage in hard plastic represents acceptable preservation with some expected losses.
