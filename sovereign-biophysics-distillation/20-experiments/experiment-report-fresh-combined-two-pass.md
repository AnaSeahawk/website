# Experiment Report: Fresh Combined Plasma — Two-Pass Distillation

**Operator:** Ana Seahawk & Olivier  
**Location:** Torremolinos, Andalusia, Spain  
**Input:** Fresh combined plasma (< 24 hours old)  
**Run structure:** Two sequential passes — Phase 1 primary distillation, Phase 2 redistillation of combined Phase 1 distillate

*Single-subject observational record. Not medical guidance.*

---

## Phase 1 — Primary Distillation (The Hydrolysis Pass)

**Goal:** Separate liquid from dense cellular debris and heavy minerals; initiate thermal breakdown of intact urea.  
**Input:** Fresh combined plasma, loaded to approximately half chamber capacity.

**Operational note:** Foam management required. Rising foam was monitored and scooped as needed during the early boiling phase to prevent overflow into the condenser. For fresh fluid, the urea hydrolysis is delayed — the alkaline spike does not appear in the first fraction but pushes into the second.

| Phase | pH | Temp (°C) | USCM | PPM / PPT | MV (ORP) | Physical & Sensory Notes |
|:---|:---|:---|:---|:---|:---|:---|
| **Baseline** | 6.4 | 31.0 | 5260 | 2690 ppm | -153 | Fresh combined plasma prior to heat. |
| **Fraction 1** | 8.8 | 26.7 | 1081 | 540 ppm | -88 | "Heads." Sweet, fresh-cut wheat aroma. Volatile flash-off. |
| **Fraction 2** | 9.4 | 33.6 | 1500 | 758 ppm | -37 | Bubbly. Strong urea/ammonia presence. Delayed hydrolysis. |
| **Residue** | 6.9 | 34.1 | 33.2k | 16.4 ppt | -81 | Thick "gravy." Extreme density of un-hydrolyzed compounds. |

**Fresh residue observation:** The retentate from fresh plasma does not behave like the aged retentate. Intact macro-proteins condense into an unusable, structurally unstable sludge — "thin gravy" — that cannot be worked with as a topical preparation. The fresh earth is biologically locked. Compare to the aged Saturn retentate documented in [Experiment: Aged Plasma Distillation →](experiment-report-aged-plasma-distillation.md).

## Phase 2 — Redistillation (The Purification Pass)

**Goal:** Bimodal separation of the volatile ammonia (synthesised during Phase 1) from the hyper-pure water body.  
**Input:** Complete combined distillate from Phase 1.

Double-distilling inverts the thermal timeline. The ammonia synthesised during Phase 1 is already present in the input and flashes off immediately as Fraction 1, clearing the path for a stable, pure water body in Fraction 2.

| Phase | pH | Temp (°C) | USCM | PPM | MV (ORP) | Physical & Sensory Notes |
|:---|:---|:---|:---|:---|:---|:---|
| **Fraction 1** | 9.6 | 41.1 | 3640 | 1820 | -42 | Spirit. Potent ammonia flash-off. |
| **Fraction 2** | 9.4 | 46.4 | 135 | 66 | +70 | Pure Plasma. Sweet, fluffy. Lunar phenomenological texture. |
| **Fraction 3** | 9.3 | 30.0 | 165 | 82 | -39 | Tails. Slight mineral bump as pot runs dry. |

**Phase 2 observation:** Stripping dissolved solids from a Phase 1 baseline of ~2690 ppm to 66 ppm in Fraction 2 eliminates the physical drag of heavy trace minerals. The resulting water carries a frictionless, floating quality on the palate distinct from the first-pass distillate.

---

## Key Observations

**The 2-pass law for fresh plasma.** Fresh biological fluid (< 24 hours old) contains large, intact protein structures that resist vaporisation. The primary challenge is thermal hydrolysis of intact urea. Once the urea flashes off as ammonia in Phase 1 and Phase 2 respectively, the water cleanly separates from the heavy protein sludge. Fresh fluid reliably yields pure water (~135 USCM) in two passes.

**Fresh vs aged retentate behaviour.** Fresh runs produce an unstable sludge retentate — not workable as a topical preparation. Aged runs produce the concentrated mineral matrix (Saturn Gold) that is the basis of the topical serum practice. The gestation process physically digests the intact proteins into water-soluble organic salts, making the retentate accessible. This is the practical function of aging beyond electrochemical changes. The divergence in retentate behaviour is one of the clearest observable differences between fresh and aged input.

**The 1/4 volume protocol** (terminating when one quarter of volume remains) applies to aged runs. For fresh runs, the residue is not retained — it is the one point in the process where the material is genuinely spent.
