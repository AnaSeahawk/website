## Key Observations

**pH circuit closes.** The native acid pH of 5.6 is present at two points only: raw prima materia (Nigredo) and vaginal output (Rubedo). Both distillate fractions are alkaline. The body restores the original signature precisely.

**ORP trajectory.** The sequence moves from positive oxidative (Albedo, +16) through reductive (Conjunctio, −28) and returns to strongly positive oxidative in the Rubedo (+78). The Rubedo ORP is the highest recorded point in the sequence.

**TDS amplification — combined output.** The ~9.5× TDS increase from Albedo to Rubedo (424 → 4,010 PPM) reflects the combined contribution of vaginal tissue exchange and urinary solutes. Because the collection was intentionally combined and the enema was fully retained, the figure cannot be disaggregated into vaginal vs. urinary fractions — it is the whole-body signal of the Rubedo as collected.

**PPB behaviour.** Zero PPB persists in the Albedo and Rubedo. The Conjunctio is the only fraction registering PPB (78). This likely reflects the saturn's concentrated mineral residue entering the second run's input — a carryover from Ana's retentate into Li's distillate.

**Conjunctio character.** The second distillate (Li's plasma run over Ana's saturn) consistently shows lower pH, negative ORP, higher conductivity, and elevated PPB relative to the first. This is coherent: the saturn loaded the input with accumulated mineral and organic material before distillation, shaping the character of the output despite the two distillates never mixing.
