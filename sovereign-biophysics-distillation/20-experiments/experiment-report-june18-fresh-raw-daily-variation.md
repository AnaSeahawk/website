# Experiment Report: June 18 — Fresh Raw Plasma Daily Variation

**Operator:** Ana Seahawk

**Date:** June 18, 2026

**Input:** Fresh raw plasma sampled at two points from the same individual

**Run structure:** Early morning collection measured around 1 PM; midday fresh collection measured within minutes; same-day material retained and later blended before distillation

---

## Placement Note

June 18 belongs to the measured archive as a source-state record.

The samples were two fresh raw readings from the same body on the same day, with all material kept within the same day's collection. The record documents within-day variation in the raw material entering the household cycle.

No distilled output was recorded in this note.

---

## Operator's Note

The interpretive turn in this record was simple: the two samples were not separate sources. They were the same body at different points in one day.

That changed the value of the readings. The record no longer asks which sample was better or what one feedstock did compared with another. It shows the raw material moving across time before it entered the shared collection. Morning and midday were not categories. They were moments in the same cycle.

The early morning sample carried a comparatively dilute profile. The midday sample arrived much denser, while pH stayed nearly still. That combination made the day feel less like a comparison table and more like a glimpse of rhythm: the body producing different concentrations without changing the basic acid-base character of the fresh material.

This is why the June 18 entry matters even without a finished distillation record. It keeps the source state alive. It records the plasma before the still made it look more stable than it may have been.

---

## Measurements

### Early Morning Sample

Collected approximately 6-7 AM and measured around 1 PM.

### Midday Fresh Sample

Collected minutes before measurement.

---

## Same-Day Comparison

| Parameter | Early Morning | Midday Fresh | Change |
| :-------- | ------------: | -----------: | -----: |
| pH        |           5.2 |          5.3 |   +0.1 |
| EC        |  3,570 µS/cm | 13,590 µS/cm |   3.8x |
| TDS       |    1,800 ppm |    6,850 ppm |   3.8x |
| ORP       |        +3 mV |       +33 mV | +30 mV |
| H₂ ppb    |            0 |            0 |      0 |

The primary signal is concentration. Conductivity and dissolved solids rose nearly fourfold between the early morning sample and the midday sample, while pH stayed almost unchanged.

ORP moved modestly further positive. Dissolved hydrogen remained undetectable in both raw fresh samples.

Because the day's material was later blended before distillation, this record does not assign those differences to separate feedstocks. It preserves the variation present in the raw source state before the still acted on the combined material.

Within the recent June record, the midday EC was higher than the June 10 Batch B fresh sample at 10,080 µS/cm and the June 13 raw menstrual morning sample at 10,690 µS/cm. The early morning June 18 sample remained much closer to the lower fresh range. The spread inside one day is therefore part of the signal.

---

## Data Check

The transcription risk in this record is unit conversion.

The midday conductivity reading was recorded as 13.59 mS/cm. Converted to microsiemens, that is 13,590 µS/cm.

The TDS values support keeping that conversion:

| Sample | EC | TDS | TDS / EC |
| :----- | -: | --: | -------: |
| Early morning | 3,570 µS/cm | 1,800 ppm | 0.50 |
| Midday fresh | 13,590 µS/cm | 6,850 ppm | 0.50 |

Both samples track the meter's approximate TDS conversion factor. The EC/TDS pairs are internally coherent.

---

## Observations

**Fresh raw plasma varied strongly within one day.** The midday sample carried far more dissolved material than the early morning sample. This difference appeared inside a single body, a single date, and a single household cycle.

**pH remained stable while concentration changed.** The pH moved only from 5.2 to 5.3 while EC and TDS rose by approximately 3.8x. In this record, concentration shifted far more than acidity.

**Timing remains a live variable.** The early morning sample was collected around 6-7 AM and measured around 1 PM. The midday sample was measured within minutes of collection. Collection timing, elapsed time before measurement, temperature, hydration, digestion, handling, and ordinary daily rhythm may all be contributing variables.

**The blended feedstock cannot be separated after the fact.** Once the day's material entered a common collection for later distillation, the two raw readings became source-state context rather than separate final-output comparisons.

---

## What Remains Open

The record opens a cleaner question for future measurements: how much of the daily raw variation comes from collection timing, and how much comes from the time between collection and measurement?

Paired fresh measurements taken immediately at several points in the day would help separate those variables. Until then, June 18 is best held as a clear within-day variation signal, not a settled timing law.
