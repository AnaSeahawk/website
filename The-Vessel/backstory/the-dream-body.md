# The Dream Body

*The distillation clears the body. The body clears the way for the dreaming. The dreaming does the work that neither diet nor chemistry can reach.*

---

Every tradition that has worked seriously with the body's own chemistry as medicine understood the dietary and physical practice as preparation, not destination. The Tibetan Six Yogas of Naropa are formally structured around this: tummo — inner heat — precedes dream yoga, because without a cleared and activated prana system, the dream state cannot be worked. The Toltec teaching says the same from a different direction: recapitulation requires a body that has freed what was bound. The shaman's soul journey requires a body sufficiently cleared to travel. The dietary work, the distillation practice, the simplification of the body's chemistry — recognised across lineages separated by centuries and continents — are the same preparation. The vessel is cleared so that something else can use it. What uses it does its primary work at night.

The dream does not bring problems to solve. What has been observed in this practice: the dreams bring something, something is received, and then the dream moves on. The release is in the receiving, not in the analysis. The Toltec teaching is precise: recapitulation is piece by piece — an encounter is retrieved, the charge released, and then it is done. Not visited again. Not elaborated. The Tibetan account from Namkhai Norbu makes the same point from inside the dream: when recognition arises, rest in it without manipulation. The content continues. The dreamer is no longer inside it in the same way. The dream brings a message to the soul, not the mind. The mind that analyses afterward is working with a residue that is already spent.

What the container would hold: there is a call, visible and growing, toward a permanent space — not only for the distillation work, not only for the personal practice, but for the kind of work that cannot be done in transit. The Egyptian incubation hall held the conditions for the practice, not merely shelter from the weather. The accumulated years of ceremony, the resonance of the stone, the quality of the silence at night — these were part of the medicine. The nomadic life keeps rebuilding from zero. Vibration cannot accumulate when the ground keeps changing. The work is moving toward a space that stays.
