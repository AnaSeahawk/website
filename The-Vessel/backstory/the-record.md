# The Record

*The practitioner's account. Written from Sarandë, Albania — April–May 2026.*

*This is not the place for the theory. The theory is in the [Sovereign Biophysics archive →](../../sovereign-biophysics-distillation/README.md). This is the place where I tell you what actually happened — in a real body, in real time.*

---

## The Arc

We left Canada in 2012. Both of us. No permanent address, no fixed country, no plan beyond the next place. Fourteen years of this.

Asia in the early years. From 2016 to 2020 we ran the Vivarium in George Town, Penang — a biophilic eco-living space that became a working prototype for what it means to inhabit a place biologically. Twenty-three moringa trees on the balconies. Kokedama hanging from every window. Bokashi soil building with guests. Probiotic enzyme making from kitchen scraps. No air conditioning in a city five degrees north of the equator. The Vivarium took 411 bookings over three and a half years and was a superhost within its first three months. It was not a business. It was a question: what does it mean to live as a biological organism in genuine relationship with a living environment?

That question was the through-line. It did not pause when we left Penang and moved through the rest of Asia, then Latin America, then eight months back in Canada, then Spain. It was the shape of the investigation, even before the investigation had found its sharpest form.

What I was not yet asking — for most of those years — was the same question about my own body. I had been taking the birth control pill since after the birth of my son in 1998. By 2011, taking it almost without interruption, specifically to avoid menstruating. It did not register as a choice in conflict with what I was investigating. I was studying how to live in regenerative relationship with biological systems — fermenting, composting, growing, observing — while chemically suppressing the most regenerative system I was carrying. The pill works by flattening the hypothalamic-pituitary-gonadal axis into silence. What I experienced as convenience was the suppression of one of the most sophisticated self-regulatory systems in biology. I barely noticed. That is how effective the suppression was.

In April 2022, in Laos, three things converged: I stopped the pill. Olivier and I went vegetarian. And on April 3rd, we began the amaroli practice in earnest — daily, committed, not as experiment but as direction. Then after about a year, we read the Damar Tantra's instructions for the matured and reduced preparation — open evaporation on the stovetop, concentrating the collected urine until what remained was thick, dark, mineral-dense. No still, no fractions. This is what we applied topically for three years, moving through rented kitchens across Asia and Latin America and eventually Spain. We started with separate waters. We began combining in 2025. By the time we ordered the countertop distiller — from an airport in Montenegro, so it would be waiting in Spain — we had built three years of practice into the body's baseline.

## February 18–19, 2026 — Torremolinos

Olivier went first.

He had aged his own plasma for the right period — catalogued as the first decan of Aquarius — and we ran the first distillation together on the evening of February 18th. Countertop distiller, a rental kitchen in Torremolinos, the Mediterranean outside the window.

The chamber foamed heavily at first — the volatile fraction breaking the surface, creating a natural overflow that separated into two distinct stages before the main distillate came through. We collected everything. Labeled everything. Measured everything.

The Stage 1 overflow: 450–466 PPM. Active, alive, fluctuating. The Stage 2 distillate in the carafe: 18 PPM. Crystal clear. The kind of clarity that water almost never has — the background visible straight through it, as through air.

The chamber residue was dark amber-brown, dense, soft to the touch. We ran it through coffee filters. The texture was like body lotion. It smelled alive. The taste was deeply umami, organically salted — and this from a body that had been strictly sodium chloride-free for over a month. The body makes its own salts. The meter couldn't measure them: it maxed out at 9999 PPM and kept reading over.

What we had been doing with the open evaporation for three years was a rougher version of the same process. The volatile compounds had been escaping into the kitchen air, the Spirit uncaptured, while the concentrated Essence and mineral retentate remained in the pot. The distiller separated what the open pot had collapsed together.

I suggested we take the distiller outside to avoid the smell in the apartment. When I mentioned it to Olivier, his eyes opened wide and he went still for a moment the way he does when something lands. *That's the best idea you've ever had.* He knew before I did that we were about to find something.

And then — before the run was even finished — the larger realisation arrived: what was coming over in the carafe was something we had not anticipated at all. The water was not a byproduct. The water was its own substance entirely.

And then all of it fell into place at once.

Every alchemical text I had read. The apparatus of Mary the Jewess. The language around the prima materia — common, despised, golden, abundant, possessed by everyone. The dietary protocols of the priests of Isis. The Buddhist Vinaya. The Damar Tantra. All of it, held in pieces for years, assembling itself in a single moment of recognition.

I came apart the way you come apart when something real arrives. Like the love that moves through a mother the moment after birth — not chosen, not managed, just there. The five thousand years of women standing behind me suddenly present, suddenly close, suddenly real in a way that no amount of reading had made them.

What I was holding was a lineage. And part of that lineage was in me — not metaphorically, not as inspiration, but as actual biological inheritance. The women of Alexandria who designed the apparatus. The women in every tradition who carried this knowledge in their bodies when the texts were burned. They were present in the chemistry of what I was holding, in the same way that my mother is present in my body, in the same way that every woman who carried me forward is present whether I name her or not.

This is my dharma. Not a burden. Not a position. A fact about what I am and what I am here to do.

## February 22 — The Preparation and the Vow

The batch I would distill had been incubating since February 1st — Sun in Aquarius, Moon in Leo. Twenty-one days of gestation. Before I ran it through the distiller I spent time with it in a way I wouldn't necessarily call ritual — but that isn't quite the right word to avoid either.

I began internally: from the sister-bottle of this same Leo Full Moon batch — same origin, same frequency — tuning the internal vessel to the external one before the heat was applied. The body remembering February 1st so that the practitioner would be an undistorted conduit for what came next.

Then the solar incubation. Two days in direct sun to awaken the Tejas within the water. Then the syphon — the vessel elevated and left undisturbed, gravity doing the first alchemical separation. Heavy sediment settling to the bottom, the clear essence rising. Only the uppermost fluid syphoned off.

And then the altar. Beneath the vessel I placed what I think of as the ancestral battery: a handmade gold bracelet from Yellowknife, gifted at nineteen, carrying my father's lineage — pharmacist and goldsmith both, chemistry and noble metals in the same hands. A citrine teardrop in gold on silver. A 20-franc gold rooster coin. Memory made material. Lineage as anchor while the heat does its work.

Then distillation.

That morning I wrote what became the founding document of this archive:

*I vow to cultivate Shen-Ming. I will observe the holographic nature of these biological waters with the precision of a balanced scale, allowing my mind to reflect exactly what is, without the sediment of personal bias.*

This is still the operating principle.

## What Changed

The body is the data. These are what I have actually noticed — framed as what they are: one practitioner's account.

The cycle returned and stabilised at 52, arriving on time, light, with a quality of interoception that years on the pill had gradually taken away. The skin changed. Sun tolerance improved. The oral ecology shifted within weeks of beginning the Solar spirit as a dental application. The dreaming intensified: more coherent, more architecturally complex, more available to waking recall. The relationship deepened in ways that have more to do with biological intimacy — whose information the body has been receiving and returning — than with anything I have adequate language for.

The diet simplified without effort. The desire for complexity fell away. The ambrosia diet — well-cooked grains, good ghee, the Lunar essence as daily drinking water — emerged naturally and has held. The body became more sensitive to external chemical input as a consequence: this sensitivity is information, not fragility. I treat it as a reading of what the body actually tolerates rather than as a problem to manage.

After four years of the base practice, three months into the distilled fractions: not a list of effects but a general direction. The body is less noisy. The signal is clearer. At 52, cycling regularly, with strong skin and a sexual life of depth and sustained vitality, after decades of pill use — I am the data. The female body is the original laboratory. It does not require permission to run its experiments. It requires only that we stop interrupting it.

## Why I Am Doing This in Public

Because the alternative is that it disappears again.

I have been away from my family for most of these fourteen years. I carry my tools — laptop, camera, microphone, distiller — and I travel light and I live simply and I work. This is the monastic form that corresponds to this practice. I did not choose it as aesthetics. It chose itself.

The women of Alexandria whose work I have spent months recovering left their knowledge in symbols because direct statement was not safe. I live in a different moment. Direct statement is possible, though it comes with its own forms of resistance — platform restrictions, automated systems that flag home laboratory work, the soft erasure of invisibility.

I am choosing to be as public as possible, precisely and specifically because I believe that what I have found is not mine to withhold. If someone in another part of the world reads this and begins their own practice and their own documentation — that is why I am writing it down.

The knowledge is in the body. Every human body. It cannot be permanently suppressed because it cannot be permanently separated from the people who carry it.

I am one person documenting one body's experience. I am also standing at the end of a very long line of women who documented theirs, in whatever form the moment permitted.

This archive is my form.
