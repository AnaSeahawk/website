# The Cord and the Curtain
## A Reclamation of the Visceral

*March 8*

The dream arrives in the frantic rhythm of departure. Luggage surrendered to a faceless porter, the favourites lost to a sudden shift in itinerary. Then, the centre-point: a figure of authority tangled in the anatomy of a woman. He holds the child, but the umbilical cord has become a noose for them both — the lifeline turned ligature. Hands move into the frame to unloop the heat of the cord from the infant. The sensation is tactile, heavy, pulsing and wet. It is the sight and touch that reality denied.

---

The history of the body is often written behind a veil. A son born breech, spine-to-spine, the cord coiled twice around the throat like a secret necklace. The delivery was a theatre of protocol. A curtain partitioned the world: on one side, the sensory void of the clinic; on the other, the unseen ocean.

Suppositories were administered without inquiry — chemical ghosts sent to haunt the nerves before the pain could even speak. The body became a variable in a management equation. When the child appeared, he was already clean, wrapped, and severed. The cord, the placenta, the amniotic brine — the entire architecture of life — was whisked away as waste. To be "fine" was to be a silent witness to one's own erasure.

The drugs wore off to finally reveal the weight of what was missing.

---

The dream is a late gift. It places the texture of the origin back into the palms. The man in the dream is not a villain, but a Pillar — the embodiment of a Five-Thousand-Year Authority, strangling himself with the very organism he seeks to control. He is as trapped in the mechanism as the mother.

There is no resolution in the waking. There is only the honesty of the unfinished.

---

In the laboratory of the self, the work is a stripping to the bone. To look at what the body produces — the plasma, the recycled urine, the mineral concentrates — is to see a sacred map that culture has reclassified as refuse. Amniotic fluid, the holy water of the first nine months, is mostly urine. It is the most sacred water a human ever inhabits, yet it is treated as a spill to be mopped from a tiled floor.

The logic is ancient and structural. The Woman is treated as a site of process, a geography to be managed rather than a participant in the rite. This is not malice; it is architecture. It is the slow, methodical siloing of the lineage carriers.

The midwives, the plant-speakers, the watchers of blood and death — their knowledge was not lost, but driven underground. It was pathologised into superstition, burned at the edges, and refined into fragments. Mothers arrived at the curtain already separated from their power, passing down a silence they inherited but never chose. *They could not gift a flame they were never allowed to hold.*

---

This is the Mother Wound that we all carry. It is not a metaphor; it is a physical transmission. It is the phantom limb of feminine gnosis.

The weight sits in the throat — the place where the word was stifled. It sits in the heart — the place where compliance was rebranded as "care." The anger and the grief are twin fires, and they are both holy. Something was stolen: the touch of the cord, the sight of the first water, the integrity of the arrival between mother and child.

The chain of the Unseen goes back into the dark, link by link, until it is felt, handled, and finally named.

---

## The Sky That Night

The Sun was deep in Pisces on March 8 — moving toward a conjunction with Mercury Retrograde and Neptune, three bodies dissolving into one another in the sign of the oceanic. The boundary between personal memory and collective inheritance was at its thinnest. What arrives under such a sky does not announce itself as vision or message. It arrives as sensation. As something the hands already know.

The Moon had entered Scorpio at the threshold of the day — Waning Gibbous, pulling inward. Scorpio governs the hidden body: the deep fluids, the reproductive mysteries, the passage between birth and death. A waning Scorpio moon over a dream of birth is the sky enacting distillation. Strip it to the bone. Find the mineral truth beneath the tissue. What remains when everything extraneous has been drawn off?

Venus was conjunct Saturn in Aries — the feminine standing before the structure. The woman before the curtain. The body held inside a management system older than any individual protocol or institution. Saturn does not ask how you feel about the arrangement. It is the arrangement.

The night held both the dissolution of Pisces and the precision of Scorpio — the oceanic and the surgical, the unseen and the mineral. It was a sky made for something that had been waiting in the fluid for decades to find its way into the hands.

The cord was always there. The sky provided the night it could finally be touched.
