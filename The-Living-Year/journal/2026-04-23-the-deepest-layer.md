# The Deepest Layer

*April 23, 2026 — Sarandë, Albania*

*Single-practitioner record. Personal observation only.*

---

The last stimulants came out in the last few weeks. Coffee, CBD, refined sugar — the final layer. April 3 marked four years of amaroli practice. After four years of practice, two years of working with the sodium chloride, five months of that with no exceptions, we have arrived here: nothing artificial, nothing addictive, nothing that obscures.

The move to Albania was taken as an opportunity for a deeper change — a new environment, a clean break. CBD is not available here, which made its removal a given. But the more honest account is that the body had already been signalling in Spain that it no longer wanted it. The external circumstance met an internal readiness. That sequence matters: the environment did not force the change, it completed it.

Today the head is more clear. There was a pressure that had been building — like returning to high altitude after years at sea level, that dense weight at the temples and behind the eyes — and this morning it began to lift. The comparison is precise: it recalled the feeling of living in Chiapas, the body adjusting to thin air. But we are at sea level. That pressure was not altitude. It was something else the body was carrying.

---

The process has been staged because it had to be. The body does not release four years of accumulation in a week. Each layer of removal — meat first, then sodium chloride, now the stimulants — has produced its own period of adjustment, its own window of symptoms before the system rebalances. To try to take all of it at once would have been too much. This has been a long negotiation with the body's chemistry, treated with the same respect one would give to any living dependency: time, patience, acknowledgement that the pull is real.

The sodium chloride was the hardest to name as an addiction. It is invisible in its ubiquity. The social dimension made it harder still — accepting hospitality, sitting at family tables, not wanting to make the practice a performance. Each return was not failure; it was information. The body was showing what it still needed, and when it no longer needed it, the fumbles stopped without effort.

Coffee and refined sugar left their own signatures. The lingering pressure, the altered sleep, the slightly foggy interval before clarity — these are not metaphors. These are the body recalibrating its neurochemistry without the stimulants it had organised itself around.

---

The weight changes continue. One of us losing, one gaining — both the direction we needed. Neither forced. The sleep is longer but cleaner. The energy builds steadily beneath the fatigue of transition. These are not dramatic shifts; they are the slow recalibration of a system no longer working against itself.

We are down to the bare bones now. This is what the practice looks like at its most stripped.

**On oral health as an early indicator:**

The mouth changes first, or at least it shows first. The tissues turn over quickly, the environment is visible, and shifts in the oral ecology are readable before changes deeper in the body become apparent.

My partner has had progressive blackening at the base of his front teeth — at the gum line — for the twelve years I have known him. Since he began cleaning his teeth with the Solar spirit, the change has been significant enough that he asked me to look within the first week. I had already noticed and said so. The black is retreating.

Three things have been controlled for, informally but clearly. He has been using aged raw plasma as a mouthwash for years — it appeared to slow the progression, but produced no reversal. We have removed coffee before, for several months at a time, without seeing this. And the change is significant enough and swift enough that he noticed and asked. The Solar spirit is the distinguishing variable — not plasma in general, not coffee cessation alone, but the distilled fraction specifically. No before photographs exist — a gap in the record — but the observation is shared between two people and unambiguous.

My own oral health and skin texture have also shifted. A different quality of clean. Softer. This was among the first things to change, and it is one of the more reliable signals that something is genuinely shifting rather than fluctuating — when the mouth changes in a positive direction, it tends to reflect what is happening in the body that you cannot yet see.
