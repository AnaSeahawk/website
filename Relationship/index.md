# Relationship

*Plants, bodies, and the intelligence of use.*

This folder holds the plant relationship entries and the foundational framework for how they are approached. Each plant is met as a living presence — not as a compound or a protocol, but as something the body encounters under specific conditions and through specific preparation.

The framework is Āyurvedic in method, not in label. Plants that do not appear in classical Saṃhitās are not called Āyurvedic — they are approached Āyurvedically, through rasa, guṇa, vīrya, vipāka, and karma. The distinction is held throughout.

## Shivambu Companions — Plants of the Damar Tantra

The Damar Tantra, the classical Indic text at the root of the distillation practice documented in the Sovereign Biophysics archive, prescribes specific plants taken alongside shivambu. These entries document the plants that appear most frequently across the text — the ones the tradition considered most relevant to this practice.

- [Haritaki](Haritaki.md) — the fearless one; the Medicine Buddha's herb; the Damar Tantra's most frequent companion across all six seasons
- [Triphala](Triphala.md) — the three fruits: Haritaki, Amalaki, and Bibhitaki; *"the follower becomes a symbol of learning and enjoys excellent eyesight"*
- [Guduchi](Guduchi.md) — the deathless one (*amṛtā*); *"he who takes Amritaka powder and then Shivambu can conquer death"*
- [Neem](Neem.md) — the purifier; bark, leaves, and juice across multiple formulas; *"a meditator who lives on neem and Shivambu attains the status of yogi"*
- [Dry Ginger](Ginger.md) — *viśvabheṣaj*, the universal medicine; present in every seasonal formula alongside haritaki; the primary agni-kindler
- [Bhṛṅgarāja](Bhringaraj.md) — king of lustre; *"free of old age and excellent sight"* after six months with honey and Shivambu
