# Relationship

*Plants, bodies, and the intelligence of use.*

This section is built around a single premise: that every plant entered this archive through direct relationship — not through a catalogue, not through a protocol, but through the body's own encounter with a living presence. The entries here are records of those encounters. They are written in the language of sensation because that is the only honest language for this work.

The approach throughout is Āyurvedic in method — observing rasa (taste), guṇa (qualities), vīrya (thermal action), and vipāka (post-digestive effect) — not because Āyurveda is the only lens available, but because it is the most precise living language for what the body actually experiences when it meets a plant. You will encounter these terms repeatedly as you read. They are not jargon to look up. They are felt in the body: *bitter* before you swallow; *heavy* in the stomach afterward; *heating* in the chest as it moves. The repetition is intentional. The learning is somatic.

This section also holds a question about origins: how did Āyurveda learn what plants do? The entries here point, collectively, toward an answer — one that the [Shivambu as Menstruum](../sovereign-biophysics-distillation/30-analysis/analysis-shivambu-as-menstruum.md) analysis develops in more depth. The Damar Tantra never presents plants alone. It presents plants *with* shivambu — always sequenced, always paired. That pairing may be the oldest clinical methodology in the archive.

## Shivambu Companions — Plants of the Damar Tantra

The Damar Tantra prescribes specific plants taken alongside shivambu. These are the ones the tradition considered most essential.

- [Haritaki](Haritaki.md) — the fearless one; the Medicine Buddha's herb; the Damar Tantra's most frequent companion across all six seasons
- [Triphala](Triphala.md) — the three fruits: Haritaki, Amalaki, and Bibhitaki; *"the follower becomes a symbol of learning and enjoys excellent eyesight"*
- [Guduchi](Guduchi.md) — the deathless one (*amṛtā*); *"he who takes Amritaka powder and then Shivambu can conquer death"*
- [Neem](Neem.md) — the purifier; *"a meditator who lives on neem and Shivambu attains the status of yogi"*
- [Dry Ginger](Ginger.md) — *viśvabheṣaj*, the universal medicine; the primary agni-kindler; present in every seasonal formula
- [Bhṛṅgarāja](Bhringaraj.md) — king of lustre; *"free of old age and excellent sight"* after six months with honey and Shivambu
